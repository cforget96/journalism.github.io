Vaudreuil-Dorion to plant nearly 11,000 trees this fall

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 5, 2022

After applying to the Natural Resources Canada's Two Billion Trees commitment program in February 2022, Vaudreuil-Dorion announced it will plant 11,000 trees in various parts of the municipality this fall. The Two Billions Trees project will be accomplished over ten years in municipalities throughout the country as part of the Canadian government's goal of attaining net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050.

To qualify for the program, each municipality must plant 10,000 trees in 2022. For Vaudreuil-Dorion, the number of trees represents a huge increase compared to past years.

"We usually plant between 600 and 800 trees a year in public places, parks and along streets," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "This fall, we are taking advantage of the program to add even more trees and thus reforest various sectors. We have made a commitment to develop a harmonious and sustainable living environment, and this includes good management of the forest cover on the territory."

The city will plant the trees on a total area of approximately 9.5 hectares spread over at least ten different sites. Six of the sites will be near urban cores, which have higher population densities.

The sites identified for high-density planting, which will be comprised of about 10,000 small trees, include Harwood nature park, the 405, Paul-Gérin-Lajoie park, Maison-Valois and Île Bray parks, the green spaces around Montée Cadieux, Route de Lotbinière, the Quinchien River sector and on the hill on Henry-Ford St.

Some 800 larger trees will be added to replace the ash trees that had to be felled due to the emerald ash borer, an insect that feeds underneath ash tree bark which can sometimes cause the trees to fall. The city was forced to cut down the trees because they were seen as safety hazards.

The total cost of the tree planting project, including land prepatarion, purchase of trees and planting work, is approximately $900,000, half of which will be covered by Natural Resources Canada. The city will decide whether it will plant another 10,000 trees in 2023 during budget preparation.
