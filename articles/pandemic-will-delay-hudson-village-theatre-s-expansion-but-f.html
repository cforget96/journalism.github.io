Pandemic will delay Hudson Village Theatre's expansion, but fundraising still going strong

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Nov 10, 2021

Although the pandemic created some obstacles, the Hudson Village Theatre has been able to continue their fundraising campaign. The group raised nearly $80,000 at an event at Whitlock golf course in mid-October.

The fundraiser will contribute to their goal of about $1 million, which they will use to match the pledge committed to the theatre by the Canadian government in June 2020. The money will go toward an expansion project that will transform the theatre into the most modern performing arts centre off the island of Montreal serving the English-speaking community.

"It's important to recognize this gem in our town. It's our cultural hub, and it brings in people from as far away as Ottawa, the Laurentians, and Montreal," said Elisabeth Corker, chair of the theatre's board of directors.

The plan was to begin construction in the fall of 2022, but with the impact the pandemic has had on construction, Corker says that building will not start before the spring of 2023.

"Construction costs have gone up over COVID, so the projected cost for the expansion has changed. We will need to commission an architect to make more detailed drawings and incorporate features that will make the building more COVID-safe," Corker said.

To this point, the board has raised close to $575,000. Kalina Skulska, the executive director of the Hudson Village Theatre, told The 1019 Report that the first phase of the project, which included connecting to the city sewage system, was now complete. She is optimistic about phase 2, when construction will begin.

"It's nice to finally be putting our shovels in the ground. We're incredibly encouraged by the support we've received from the community, so it's nice to be able to show them that their support has paid off," Skulska said.

Although the theatre was closed for 16 months during the pandemic, they resumed programming in August. They will continue to host notable acts like Bowser and Blue, who will perform a four-show engagement at the theatre in early December.
