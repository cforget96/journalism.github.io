Whitlock Golf Club member pays it forward with youth golf scholarship

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · May 25, 2022

Graham Smith has a disarming aura about him. He carries himself with a kind, calm confidence that instills trust. It should come as no surprise, then, that Smith is one of the top dog trainers in the world. He has won the Canadian "Trainer of the Year' award four times and the "Leader of the Pack" award twice, a worldwide distinction.

Smith has reached the peak of career success, but fifteen months ago, Smith lost his mother, Jane Atkinson, who played an invaluable role in guiding Smith to where he is today.

"Everyone says their mother is the best, but she was a very special person," he remembers. She raised me as a single mother and was always charity-oriented, even with our small means."

After her passing, Smith and his wife, Kathy Holland, wanted commemorate his mother. She loved the outdoors and was passionate about golf, so last year Smith donated two fountains in her honor to the Whitlock Golf Club, where he has been a member for the last 25 years.

This year, Smith has gone a step further. He decided to offer two local boys memberships at Whitlock---complete with new clubs, balls, gloves, clothes, shoes, food credit, and private lessons with Gregg Cuthill, the course pro.

"I thought of the people who gave me breaks in my life. I wanted to pick up my mother's torch and pay it forward," Smith said.

Last January, Smith reached out to Patrick Mischook, the physical education teacher at Westwood Senior High School, to help him find a good fit for the scholarship.

"Initially, I asked that he send me one candidate," said Smith. "When he sent me two, I looked over their profiles and they were exactly the kind of boys I was looking for---polite young men, honour roll students who loved golf. I couldn't possibly decide to only pick one of them. So, I thought 'What would mom do?' and decided to give scholarships to both of them."

The recipients of the scholarship are Xander Derix, 15, and Brandon Boros, 16. Both boys will have an opportunity to get the full golf experience, complete with a job at the course, which Smith asked Whitlock president, Dennis Debragga, to help him with.

Denise Skordakis, Xander's mother, says her son gets his passion for golf from his grandfather.

"He got into golf about four years ago," she said. "My father-in-law is a big golfer, and he started bringing Xander to different courses with him. Xander immediately took to it, so much so that his grandfather called me and said that Xander was getting better than him, so he suggested he take some lessons to continue improving. Now, we can't get him off the course."

Xander and Brandon met with Smith and Debragga last Friday at the Club to pick up their gear for the season.

"They're both very respectful boys---they called me 'Mr. Smith.' When I told them that my name is Graham, they started calling me 'Mr. Graham,'" Smith laughingly recalled. "We need more people like them around."

This is Smith's first year offering the scholarship, but he hopes to continue the program in his mother's memory. Next year, he plans to offer the Jane Atkinson-Whitlock scholarship to a local girl.
