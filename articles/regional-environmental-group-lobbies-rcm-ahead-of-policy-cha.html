Regional environmental group lobbies RCM ahead of policy changes

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Undated

As officials for the RCM (regional county municipality) of Vaudreuil-Soulanges are revising development policies, citizen-led environmental activist group M.A.R.E. (Mouvement d'Action Régional en Environment) sees an opportunity to influence the government's approach to environmentalism in the region.

In a July 12 press release, the group outlined three proposals they discussed with the RCM:

To assess the natural environment of the region in order to establish an effective conservation plan, to establish a conservation target of 17% of the territory, and to establish a reforestation plan to increase forest cover to nearly 50%.

Many of the region's plans, including waste management, wetlands, urban development, and wooded areas, are under revision. According to M.A.R.E. spokesperson David Pharand, the group hopes to hold RCM officials accountable and for their proposals to be seriously considered. The RCM is currently about to adopt its new development plan, which is why M.A.R.E. is taking action.

"There is an urgency present. We can't afford to only act in 5 or 8 years," said Pharand. The group is particularly concerned with the rate of urban sprawl and agricultural expansion in the region. Only 1.4% of the region's territory is protected, and the group believes the RCM should be able to bring that up to 17%, a goal that has been fulfilled by "other levels of government," according to their press release.

M.A.R.E. works closely with the 13 other environmental initiatives in the region. While the other groups focus on specific issues (for example, the Pincourt Vert group was dedicated to preserving the Rousseau Wetlands in 2020), M.A.R.E. is fighting for environmental protection across the region. The group stated that its proposals were made on behalf of all the other groups in the region.

The RCM responded to M.A.R.E. by saying they will continue to gather knowledge of their natural spaces and to improve their territory planning tools, but Pharand foresees some difficulties ahead. Getting all 23 municipalities in the region to change their policies will take time and patience.

"There are a lot of representatives that want to do something, but not everyone is on the same page. The RCM must make changes to its behaviour and regulations before it's too late," Pharand said.

In a written statement, RCM communications spokesperson Marie-Eve Gingras-Gosselin explained that they would be working on new environmental plans starting in the fall. The RCM has set itself the goal of reducing its greenhouse gas emissions by 13% by 2026.

Despite the RCM's apparent willingness to work with M.A.R.E., the group is concerned that without serious modification to their development plans, it may not be enough.
