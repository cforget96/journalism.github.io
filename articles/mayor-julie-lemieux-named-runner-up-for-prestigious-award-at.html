Mayor Julie Lemieux named runner-up for prestigious award at Quebec Municipal Congress

By Christopher Forget · Oct 5, 2022

It may have not been the result she had hoped for, but Très-Saint-Rédempteur Mayor Julie Lemieux still considers the Fédération québécoise des municipalités (FMQ) congress to be a great success.

"Many delegates attended the panel and the LGBTQ2+ Vaudreuil-Soulanges booth," Lemieux said. "I received a lot of appreciation after the conference, people came to see me to tell me about their satisfactions and their interests, and we were able to make many connections to raise awareness of the realities of LGBTQ2+ community."

Lemieux became the first trans mayor in Canada when she was elected in 2017, and became the first in the world to be re-elected during last years' municipal election.

The Jean-Moreau-Moreau award, which recognizes the exceptional commitment, leadership, and dynamism of an elected municipal official in their community, was granted to Steven Larose, the mayor of Montcalm.

Despite the disappointment, Lemieux sees her nomination as a win for the entire LGBTQ2+ community, specifically in Vaudreuil-Soulanges.

"I am very grateful for my colleagues from the municipality and the MRC for your support in working for inclusion and diversity," she said. Thank you for having seen in me what I did not perceive myself."
