West Island native Jordan Dumais drafted to Columbus Blue Jackets

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 12, 2022

After weeks of anticipation, Jordan Dumais finally lived his dream of being drafted into the NHL on July 8. The 18-year-old Beaconsfield native was taken by the Columbus Blue Jackets 96th overall, the final pick of the third round.

Dumais just came off an impressive season with the Halifax Mooseheads of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey league. After being awarded the QMJHL Most Sportsmanlike Player, he was named the Sportsmanlike Player of the entire CHL, the governing body of the Quebec, Ontario, and Western Major Junior leagues.

He also compiled 109 points in 68 games, good for 3rd in league scoring. His point totals were the most for a draft-eligible Quebec league player since Sidney Crosby in the 2004-2005 season.

Despite his immense skill and high hockey IQ, many teams passed on Dumais because of his size. Standing at 5-foot-8 and 165 pounds, competing at hockey's highest level will be a challenge for Dumais. For comparison, the Montreal Canadiens selected Juraj Slafkovsky, a 6-foot-4, 230-pound Slovak, with the first overall pick.

But many think that Dumais is up for the challenge. Skeptics need not look any further than the undersized and overlooked star who currently coaches the Montreal Canadiens, Martin St. Louis.

St. Louis, who grew up playing hockey with Dumais' father, Patrice, was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame despite never being drafted. Before the draft began, he took to the Bell Centre stage to give some words of encouragement to the anxious prospects.

"Understand that [being drafted ] is just the beginning. It's probably the easy part," he said before a crowd of fans, prospects and their families. "Just keep pushing. You match work ethic with will, you can accomplish anything."

Dumais attended the Blue Jacket's development camp from July 11-13 and will return to Columbus for the team's rookie camp in September.
