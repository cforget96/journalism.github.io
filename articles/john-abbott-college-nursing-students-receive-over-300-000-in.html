John Abbott College Nursing students receive over $300,000 in funding

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Mar 2, 2022

Last week, 180 John Abbott Nursing students were shocked to discover that they would each be receiving grants of $1,800 for a total of $324,000.

"It was a day like any other until we got the news. It came as a huge surprise," said Nursing student and Hudson resident Alison Moore.

The grant was funded in part by the Government of Canada and CEWIL, a national organization whose mission is to create future-ready students through work-integrated learning initiatives.

Nursing department co-chairperson Ute Beffert applied for the funding to help with the additional stresses this cohort of students has experienced as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic.

"Given the amazing commitment it takes to be a nurse these days and the stress involved in this program, I felt, if we had a shot at this funding, why not give it a go and see if we can help these students become more successful?" said Beffert.

Beffert added that the pandemic has required students to begin their stages in hospitals earlier than usual, which has thrust students into the realities of working in healthcare, including seeing people die from COVID-19.

Aside from the emotional distress, Nursing students also carry a financial burden. Beffert expects many of the students will use the grant for transportation, tuition, and books.

"Everyone assumes that all students from the West Island come from an affluent background, but this simply isn't the case," she said.

"It's no secret that the program gets expensive," said Moore. "The grant shows me that we are supported by our teachers and by the funding organizations, which is a nice feeling."

Moore has had stages at three hospitals: the Veteran's hospital in St. Anne de Bellevue, Lakeshore General and St. Mary's, the latter for which she drove from Hudson to Namur metro station, then took the metro the rest of the way. She said it has been a pleasure to help out on the front lines, but it was a big jump.

"I started out taking classes online, so it was a bit nerve-wracking going directly into hospitals without being able to completely hone our skills first," she said.

Moore said she will receive the first installment of the grant this week, with the second one coming later in the semester.
