Quebec government announces 425 new spaces in educational childcare centres in Vaudreuil-Soulanges

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Feb 16, 2022

Quebec Minister of Family Mathieu Lacombe announced on February 10 that the educational childcare service network will be adding nearly 14,000 places throughout the province, 425 of which will be in the Vaudreuil-Soulanges region by March 2025.

These are the first results of the ongoing call for projects launched last October, an initiative that aims to ensure that every child in Quebec will have access to daycare.

There are currently 11 projects underway in the region in the municipalities of Hudson, Vaudreuil-Dorion, St. Lazare, St. Zotique, St. Clet, Coteau-du-Lac, and Pincourt. While the bulk of these projects involve renovations that will allow for an increased capacity at existing locations, three of the projects will be entirely new centres, each creating 100 spots. One of the centres will be in Pincourt; the other two will be in St. Lazare.

"It 's great news for our families. These are children we know around us who will be able to develop their full potential. But above all, parents will be able to return to work and help our economy," said MNA for Soulanges Marilyne Picard.

The announcement comes just three months after the government officially launched its call for projects. The press release emphasized that the government has never allocated so many spaces so quickly, calling it the most ambitious undertaking since the childcare network was created, which they hope will address the urgent need for more spaces.

"Our region had a critical shortage of subsidized spaces. Today 's announcement is great news for families in the region who will soon be able to take advantage of these new spaces in our child care services. The situation for parents of young children is not easy right now,

and every step we take to address the shortage of spaces should be celebrated," said Minister of Montérégie Simon Jolin-Barrette.

In order to keep families up to date on the ongoing initiative, which is still 3000 spots short of its goal of 17,000, Family Quebec has shared a list of projects underway on its website.
