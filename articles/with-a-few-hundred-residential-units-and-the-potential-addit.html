With a few hundred residential units and the potential addition of a cinema on the way, the Faubourg de L'Ile looks to become a "service-oriented" mall

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 13, 2021

For years now, there have been rumblings about what might become of the Fauboug de L'Ile in Pincourt. Until now, though, there have been many questions and far too few answers.

In a recent conversation with The 1019 Report, Groupe Quint, the owners of the mall, announced that a new makeover is on the horizon.

"People like to have mixed uses in a space. That's what we want to create with the Faubourg," said Ian Quint, the group's president.

Quint stated that the old Walmart location will be demolished to make room for several hundred residential units in the form of condos and multi-tenant homes. Groupe Quint will have a sales office set up in the mall by the end of the year.

The construction of these residential units, in addition to the apartment complexes that were recently put up on the corner of Cardinal-Léger boulevard and 5th avenue, will surely increase foot traffic in the area, which is great news for the mall's tenants.

In addition to the residential units, Groupe Quint is also in communication with the city of Pincourt to move the community library, which is currently located at Chêne-Bleu Secondary School, into the mall. The group is also in "advanced talks" with a cinema operator to finally bring a movie theatre to the city. After discussions with Cinema Guzzo fizzled out a few years ago, Quint is confident that the group will come to an agreement with their new partner in 2022.

The addition of these services would move the mall away from being a "fashion mall." Although Quint explained that the mall did not lose any tenants over the pandemic, the cultural shift has been devastating for the boutiques. The mall's essential services, on the other hand, went relatively unaffected.

"The mall is already very service-oriented; places like Dollarama, Pharmaprix, RBC, the dental office and the optometry office will always be necessary for clients," Quint said.

The success of the government-operated cannabis store, the SQDC, shows that there is still a demand for in-person service, while many people now do the bulk of their shopping online. The pandemic accelerated this shift, and Groupe Quint has recognized it.

"The future of the Faubourg de L'Ile will be services, not fashion retailers. If the municipal services relocate, there will only be a few other small spaces left to rent. In the immediate future, we're focused on construction, but we're excited for what will come next," said Quint.
