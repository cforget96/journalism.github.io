Tremblay wins VSLL election in second attempt at office

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Nov 10, 2021

Mario Tremblay defeated incumbent mayor of Vaudreuil sur le lac Philip Lapalme in last Sunday's election, and Tremblay is already hard at work. His first order of business? To bring cohesion to city council.

"The past few years have been tumultuous, but I'm confident that we can create a solid council---one with collegiality and who listens to the population," said Tremblay in an interview with The 1019 Report, referring to when the city lost quorum in February 2020 after the resignation of several council members and the former mayor, Claude Pilon.

Tremblay has run for office before; in the fall of 2020, he lost to Lapalme in a byelection. This time around, Tremblay won with 378 votes to Lapalme's 337 votes. Voter turnout was high in the village of 1,400, with nearly 70% of the eligible population participating in the election.

"We did a lot of door-to-door campaigning, so that may have had an effect on the turnout. It also shows an interest in local politics, which is always great to see," Tremblay said.

Before moving into municipal politics, Tremblay worked in pharmaceuticals as VP of sales for the Canadian division of AtraZeneca. He looks to bring that high-level experience to his mayoral term.

"I always been able to create a good working atmosphere. I feel like it is a strength of mine. I'm excited to begin working with council, who also have a lot of diverse experience," said Tremblay, who was elected along with 5 new councillors.

Tremblay plans to continue working on the projects the previous regime had started, most notably the acquisition of a 20-hectare wooded area from private landowners. The preservation of the woods has been an important point in the previous two elections.

Tremblay also emphasized his intentions to make the town more accessible to its anglophone residents. He hopes he and his council will reflect the population, which is becoming more diverse.

"This municipality is one of the best and most beautiful places to live in the Montreal area. My objective is to create a place we're all proud to live in," he said.
