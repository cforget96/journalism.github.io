Case of distemper discovered in Pincourt skunk

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Jan 5, 2022

Distemper is a virus often found in skunks and raccoons in southern Quebec, but can sometimes be found in foxes and coyotes, too. Infected animals display neurological symptoms---convulsions, confusion, weakness, and reduced mobility---all of which are also observed in animals with rabies. The difference between the two viruses is that animals with distemper also catch pneumonia.

"Distemper is endemic in southern Quebec, meaning it is always present in the area. It is quite common, and some epidemics pop up in clusters on occasion," explained Professor Stéphane Lair, Director of the Quebec branch of the Canadian Wildlife Health Cooperative.

Lair added that distemper is much more common in Quebec than rabies. Cases of rabies in raccoons and skunks began to emerge in the early 2000s after being introduced from the United States, which is why wildlife workers are specifically concerned about cases of distemper in Quebec's southern region. There haven't been any reported cases of rabies in the past decade aside from one isolated case in 2015.

Lair also warned that distemper can effect dogs, but that the vaccine against distemper is part of the standard vaccination of dogs across North America. Very few cases of distemper in dogs have been reported due to the high vaccination rate, but Lair stressed the importance of ensuring that owners protect their pets against the virus.

Any sighting of skunks or raccoons exhibiting bizarre physical or neurological behaviour should contact the rabies surveillance and control operations hotline: 1-877-346-6763.
