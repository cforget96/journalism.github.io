Vaudreuil-Dorion offers a weekend of "Feux et Flocons"

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 2, 2022

After taking a year off in 2021 due to COVID-19 health restrictions, the popular "Feux et Flocons" outdoor music festival will return this year in a different format. The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion will host the event on March 4 and 5, starting at 7 p.m., at Maison-Valois Park.

The 2020 edition of the event took place one week before the pandemic hit Quebec. That event set a new record, with nearly 5000 people in attendance. Considering its popularity, city officials are making sure this year's edition will be COVID-safe.

"At the time of organizing the event, the health measures in effect were much more restrictive than they are today, so we decided to hold Feux et Flocons over two days to allow as many citizens as possible to enjoy the activity, " explains Vincent Bastien, director of Recreation and Cultural Services.

DJ Yan Gagnon will be spinning tunes from the 80s and 90s on Friday, while DJ Dan Desnoyers will play songs from the 2000s on Saturday. To top things off, fireworks will light up the sky on both nights at approximately 9:00 pm.

Attendees will have the option to eat from a food truck, and a bar will be serving hot and alcoholic drinks. Vaccinations passports and proof of identity will be required to access the site.

This city notes that unfavorable weather conditions could cause the event to be cancelled. Updates about the status of the event will be posted on the city 's Facebook page.
