West Island native shows promise ahead of NHL Entry Draft

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jun 15, 2022

18-year-old Jordan Dumais hopes to hear his name called at the 2022 NHL Entry Draft in July. As the biggest day of his life approaches, he credits his West Island roots as a part of his success.

"The West Island was a great place to grow up, especially if you love hockey," the Beaconsfield native recalled. "There are tons of rinks. I used to play on them with my friends all the time."

After coming through the ranks in the Lac Saint Louis minor hockey system, Dumais was drafted by the Halifax Mooseheads of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League. He is coming off a stellar season for the Mooseheads, and many draft analysts now consider him to be a potential steal for whichever NHL team drafts him. He compiled 109 points in 68 games last season, good for third in the league. What's most impressive is Dumais accomplished the feat at just 17 years old in a league where the dominant players are often two or even three years his senior.

Standing at 5'9, Dumais is undersized by NHL standards---but he doesn't let that deter him. He tries to model his play after NHL legend Patrick Kane, whose potential for success was also questioned due to his small frame.

"I idolized [Kane ] growing up," Dumais said. "I think I play a bit like him too, especially his playmaking and skill."

Dumais was the 2022 recipient of the Frank J. Selke Memorial Trophy, awarded to the most sportsmanlike player in the QMJHL. Over the course of the season, he committed three minor penalties for a total of just six penalty minutes. Dumais believes he has the most success when he is able to balance intensity with poise.

"I always try to play hard and get into the dirty areas," he said. "But it's important to play smart and not lose your temper. I've really developed my defensive game this season because the league is so fast. You don't want to make a lazy defensive play and cost your team a penalty."

The Selke Trophy win is a testament to Dumais' maturity, which is something most NHL teams will consider come draft day. But although he was ranked 73rd on the final edition of the NHL Central Scouting list, he was not among the 96 prospects invited to the NHL combine in May.

Despite this disappointment, Dumais is confident he can contribute on hockey's biggest stage.

"I'm built for hockey," he said. "I really think I can help a team with my competitiveness, my hockey IQ, and my offensive abilities.

The NHL Entry Draft will be held at the Bell Centre on July 7 and 8.
