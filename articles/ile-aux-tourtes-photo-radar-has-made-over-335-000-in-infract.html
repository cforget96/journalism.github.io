Ile aux Tourtes photo radar has made over $335,000 in infractions a month since November

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Apr 27, 2022

With the construction on the Ile-aux-Tourtes bridge set to continue until 2027, traffic is not the only thing motorists should be concerned about.

After the emergency closure of the bridge in May 2021, a mobile photo radar was installed on either side of the bridge. Since then, it has recorded nearly 8,000 infractions, with the average ticket cost totaling about $300. The average ticket is higher around the bridge compared to the photo radar stop in Pincourt because it is in a construction area.

In September 2021, The 1019 Report examined details of the infractions in the months following the bridge's reopening. The results were astonishing---$757,220 in infractions were given out in June and July alone.

Now, with a larger sample size, these numbers have proven to be the rule, not the exception. After 769 tickets were handed out in August, infractions nearly came to a complete halt in September and October, with only 12 infractions in a 60-day period.

After this lull, monthly numbers shot back up; an average of $335,000 and 1,157 infractions have been recorded around the bridge each month since November. The single-month high was in December, when a staggering 581,330 was collected from 2,060 infractions.

COMMENT FROM GILLES PAYER

Pincourt photo radar numbers stable since emergency bridge closure

While the data Vaudreuil mobile photo radar data has been full of spikes and valleys, the Pincourt radar trap has been consistent since traffic being was rerouted to Highway 20 during the Ile aux Tourtes bridge closure in May 2021.

After eclipsing 1000 infractions in both June and July, the average number of monthly infractions has hovered around 800 at the permanent photo radar trap.

The average ticket price since early August is $124---less than half the average ticket price at the Ile aux Tourtes radar. The fines are likely less severe at the Pincourt radar because of the lower speed limit and the fact it is not in a construction zone.

The Ministry of Transportation has begun to conduct preliminary studies to improve mobility in the Highway 20 corridor between Route 342 in Vaudreuil-Dorion and the Galipeault Bridge in Ile Perrot, which could change how the photo radar is used moving forward.

40 per cent of infractions at St. Lazare photo radar came in September alone

The St. Lazare mobile radar, which is located near the corner of Cite des Jeunes Blvd. and Ste. Angelique Rd., has only been used intermittently over the last eight months.

A total of 511 infractions have been recorded since July, with the average fine at $160.

The bulk of tickets came in the fall, with 191 coming in September, nearly 40 per cent of all infractions. For comparison, there have only been 48 infractions in St. Lazare since the start of 2022.
