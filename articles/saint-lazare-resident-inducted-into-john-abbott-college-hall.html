Saint Lazare resident inducted into John Abbott College Hall of Distinction

By Christopher Forget · Sep 7, 2022

For decades, Bob McEwen has been a consistent presence in the John Abbott College community. That's why he's among the 11 JAC alumni to be inducted in the College's Hall of Distinction, a group of 50 people who were nominated to commemorate the 50 years since the college first opened in 1971.

Though he has been at the heart of JAC life for five decades, that almost changed when his own heart nearly cost him his life. In December of 2021, McEwen went in cardiac arrest that may have been fatal, were it not for the help of his family and the first responders to save him.

"I shouldn't be here talking to you," McEwen said. "My daughter and my wife are my heroes."

When news of his health scare became public, thousands of people reached out to him personally to check in via email and on Facebook. The online reaction alone indicates how many lives he's touched through his many projects and initiatives, and why he is being inducted into the Hall of Distinction.

"Thousands of people have come through JAC over the years, so I'm totally honoured to be one of the 50 inductees" McEwen said."

Although he grew up in Ormstown, McEwen has been a Sainte Anne's transplant for most of his adult life. He first enrolled at John Abbott in 1973, which is where he met Marg, his wife of 46 years. McEwen eventually got a job at Macdonald Campus, the McGill property adjacent to John Abbott, where he taught practical and theoretical lessons in the poultry farming department. He lived on campus with his family for most of his life before moving to Saint Lazare ten years ago.

Throughout his career, McEwen has been a leader and role model to thousands of young adults on campus. He became the head coach of the John Abbott men's hockey team in the late 1990s. In 2006, he co-founded the school's Remembrance Day ceremony and has organized it ever since.

"I never gave it a lot of thought before I received the call to be inducted, but John Abbott and Macdonald Campus has been my life. I got there as a young kid and never left," he said.

Outside of school, McEwen has given back to the community through his own means. In 1997 he started the Mingo McEwen Fund with a charity hockey game between John Abbott and Lennoxville. 25 years later, the fund has raised over a million dollars, donating to organizations like the Montreal Children's Hospital, West Island Palliative Care, and the Ste. Anne de Bellevue Veteran's hospital. Through it all, John Abbott remained an influence in McEwen's charity work.

"The MMF colors are blue and gold, which obviously was influenced by the John Abbott colours," he said.

McEwen is also a partner at Cunningham's Pub in Ste. Anne's, where he can often be spotted chatting with patrons and working with the staff.

"I love being in the kitchen, the atmosphere. It's peaceful for me," he said.

The induction ceremony will take place in the Stewart Hall building at John Abbott on September 15. All 50 inductees into the Hall of Distinction were nominated by a committee of peers, including colleagues and former classmates.

"The plan is to continue to induct one person into the Hall of Distinction each year," said John Abbott Communications and Media Relations Officer Debbie Cribb.

Though McEwen said he was humbled to be inducted, those who have met him know he earned the distinction through sharing his time and touching so many lives along the way.

"I love getting involved. I think I get that from my parents. And over the years I've gotten to meet a lot of amazing people and have gotten so many great experiences because of it."
