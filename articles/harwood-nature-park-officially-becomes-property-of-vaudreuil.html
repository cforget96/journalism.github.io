Harwood nature park officially becomes property of Vaudreuil-Dorion

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Undated

Formerly known as the Harwood Golf course, the nature park on Harwood Blvd. is now open to the public.

The park will intermittently be called the Harwood nature park, but that name could change as the project develops.

The over two million square-foot park open between 7 A.M. and 11 P.M. For the time being, the park will include basic equipment such as tables, garbage cans, bike racks, and restrooms. The city will take the next year to decide on larger-scale plans for the space.

"Our vision is to keep the park natural, but we want to include a few different sectors within the space. There could be a dog park, or maybe even a mini soccer field for children. In the winter, it can be used for snowshoeing or cross-country skiing. We're also considering renting out the clubhouse, which has a kitchen and a bar, so there are many possibilities," Mayor Guy Pilon told The 1019 Report.

Pilon added that additions to the park this year will be very minimal. The city plans to discuss the future of the park in more detail this fall.

"The plan can evolve over the next 3 to 5 years. We want to learn more about how Vaudreuil-Dorion residents want to use the space," he added.

In an August 6 press release, the city stated that the acquisition of the park will ensure the preservation of another green space, along with l'Île Bray park, the 405 park on Saint-Charles Street, and the land purchased from the Pères de Sainte-Croix.

The acquisition of the land coincides with the city's five-year plan to improve environmental harmony and sustainability.
