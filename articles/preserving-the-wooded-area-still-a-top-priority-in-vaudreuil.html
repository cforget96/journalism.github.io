Preserving the wooded area still a top priority in Vaudreuil-sur-le-Lac mayoral race

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Oct 27, 2021

When voters in the small town of Vaudreuil sur le Lac go to the polls on Nov. 7, it will be a little bit like Groundhog Day -- the movie, that is.

Their choice of candidates running for mayor will be almost the same as it was in a by-election just last year. And the central issue of the campaign has not changed -- how to preserve a nearly 20-hectare forested area from being developed.

Although they have worked closely with incumbent mayoral candidate Philip Lapalme over the last year, the group that succeeded in focusing everyone's attention in the small town of 1370 residents has opted not to take sides in the election. Both mayoral candidates are promising to save the woods from development, so the group is leaving the decision about who will complete that task up to the voters.

"Our group does not align itself with any candidate," said Marie-Agnès Huberlant, a spokesperson for the Regroupement pour la protection du Boisé du VSLL.

"The mission of the group is to protect the woods, not to be involved in politics. We aim to inform fellow citizens about the positions of each candidate, which we do by asking them questions and sharing their answers with the public," Huberlant added.

Lapalme, who won last year's by-election by a slim 44-vote margin, has vowed to continue his work on acquiring the entire wooded area from private owners. His efforts came as a relief for residents who were worried by the municipal government's inaction under interim mayor André Bélanger.

"It was important for me to put together resources to get this project off the ground and to accelerate the process of preserving the woodland. Our fast action allowed us to find financial partners, complete the market evaluation of the land covering the entire woodland, and begin talks with the landowners. In 10 months, the project has made great strides," said Lapalme in an Oct. 19 press release.

Lapalme is again being challenged by Mario Tremblay. Tremblay ran in the in the 2020 by-election, coming in last, behind Lapalme and a third candidate, Michel Boisvert. Boisvert is not running in the current election.

When it comes to acquiring the wooded area from the landowners, Tremblay, who previously worked as VP of sales for the Canadian division of pharmaceutical company AstraZeneca, hopes to bring his experience to the negotiating table, and transparency to the village's residents.

"Two thirds of the cost to purchase the woods will be covered by funding, but the exact cost has not been shared with the public yet. If we don't know how much the land will cost, it's difficult to have an understanding of how far along we are in the whole process. My plan would include making sure we get funding from whoever can help and explore all options," said Tremblay.

Regardless of who is elected, the citizen-led group will undoubtedly continue to hold municipal officials accountable and remind them about the importance of the village's beloved woods.
