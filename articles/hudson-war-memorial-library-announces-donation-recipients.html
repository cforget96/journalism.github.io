Hudson War Memorial Library announces donation recipients

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Dec 8, 2021

The War Memorial Library, located at 60 Elm St. in Hudson Heights, has announced the organizations it will be donating to this holiday season.

The library, which is a registered charity, is volunteer-run and receives no federal, provincial or municipal funding. The cost of maintaining the collection and the building is met by memberships, donations, and book sales.

Every Saturday morning between 9 and 12, the library holds a sale of donated goods at the Bunker, an area located in the basement of the library.

"A very dedicated group of volunteers work throughout the week to prepare the Saturday sale. 40 cents on every dollar made at the Bunker is donated to a list of charities voted on by the Library Board," explained Celia Joslin, co-chair of the library.

The library donates their profits twice a year---once at Christmas, and again in April. Joslin would not specify the total amount they will be donating this holiday season.

In normal times, the library holds a Christmas gathering to hand out the cheques. This year, they will mail the cheques the recipients.

This year's recipients are:

Nova Day Center

LePont/Bridging

V-S Health Care Foundation (Hudson Palliative Care)

La Passerelle Shelter

Hudson Fireman 's baskets

Hudson Historical Society

Alzheimer Society Suroit

Quebec Veterans Foundation (Ste-Anne-de-Bellevue Hospital Fund)

Valleyfield Hospital Foundation

Hawkesbury Hospital Foundation

Literacy Unlimited

Centre d 'Action Benevole Vaudreuil-Dorion

First Nations Child and Family Services

Le Cafe de la Debrouille
