Quebec hockey committee recommends teaching ice skating in elementary schools

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jun 8, 2022

Hockey has been synonymous with Quebec culture since 1875, when the first-ever organized hockey game was played in Montreal. However, a recent decline in interest has prompted the provincial government to take steps to revitalize the sport.

Hockey should be named Quebec's official national sport and elementary schools in the province should teach ice skating as part of the curriculum. These are two of the more than 60 recommendations a special committee has made to Premier François Legault.

The Quebec Hockey Development Committee, created in November 2021 in an attempt to increase the number of Quebec players drafted to the National Hockey League, submitted its report last month with a long list of suggestions on how to get more kids involved in the game.

"These proposals will form the foundation for long-term, positive change for the benefit of Quebec 's youth in field hockey," said Marc Denis, formerly an NHL goaltender and the chairperson of the committee.

The committee, which is made up of retired male and female hockey stars, stressed that for the game to grow, hockey needs to be safe, accessible and fun for young boys and girls.

The first of these recommendations was to teach ice skating in schools. The committee cited Australia as an example of a nation that teaches swimming as a part of its primary school curriculum. It would also expose young Quebecers to skating when they may not otherwise have the chance due to financial or cultural barriers.

They also recommend rolling back competition in youth hockey, which can be stressful on children. The committee proposed that reducing the number of tournaments for children under 11 years of age and eliminating standings and statistics for age groups of 13 and under would be good ways to achieve this.

Lastly, the committee proposed to launch a vast project to build covered and refrigerated outdoor rinks in all regions of Quebec, making hockey accessible to communities across the province.

In the report, Denis clarified that this would the first of many to restore Quebec to its former hockey glory.

"This report does not represent an end in itself," he said, "but rather the beginning of a new era for our national sport, a first step towards a promising future, the initial stage of a great project."
