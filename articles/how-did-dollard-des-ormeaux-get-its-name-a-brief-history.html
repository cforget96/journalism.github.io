How did Dollard-des-Ormeaux get its name?: A brief history

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 14, 2022

To most people in the West Island, Dollard-des-Ormeaux is simplify referred to in its acronym form: DDO. But at one time, the town went by a different name.

Originally, the area was a part of the Parish of Ste-Geneviève. It only became a distinct municipality in 1924, when the parish imposed taxes for road improvements on Gouin Blvd, which the residents of DDO refused to pay, which explains why the boulevard does not run through the municipality today.

Inspired by the steadfastness and fiery spirit of the residents, a notary named Ernest Jasmin suggested that the town be name Dollard-des-Ormeaux to pay homage to Adam Dollard-des-Ormeaux, the French garrison commander who died in a battle with the Haudenosaunee to protect the Ville-Marie settlement in 1660.

By the time Jasmin recommended the name, Dollard-des-Ormeaux had become an iconic, almost mythic historical figure in the French-Canadian imagination. He was used by 20th-centruy nationalists like Lionel Groulx as an example of the bravery and heroism exhibited by the early French colonists, and thus became a symbol of national pride at a time when the English population was becoming more powerful in Quebec society.

While DDO is probably the biggest tribute to the historic figure, there are plenty of other examples of streets, lakes, parks and rivers all named in Dollard-des-Ormeaux's honor. Dollard Ave. in Lasalle is one example, while a statue in Parc Lafontaine and an engraving of his death at Place d'Armes both pay homage to the man.
