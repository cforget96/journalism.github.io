St. Zotique's new "2e Envol" project gives used items a second life

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 16, 2022

The municipality of St. Zotique wants to turn your trash into someone else's treasure. In partnership with the MRC of Vaudreuil-Soulanges, the city is launching a workshop-boutique pilot project that will transform and resell refurbished furniture and hardware items to citizens at low prices.

Dubbed "2e Envol," the project will allow for carpentry and cabinetmaking students from the Centre intégré du Nouvel-Envol in Salaberry-de-Valleyfield to hone their restoration and transformation skills. The practice will also help students integrate into the job market once they complete their studies.

"We are aware of the environmental values of the citizens of our community, many of whom share a passion for the environment. Our 'Éco St-Zo' page on has over twelve hundred members, so we know there is an interest in environmentalism. So far, the citizen support has been enormous," said St. Zotique mayor Yvon Chiasson.

The municipality hopes that the new service, located at the St. Zotique Eco Center, will help reduce the consumption of new goods by giving a second life to functional goods that simply need a bit of sprucing up.

The municipality will be using two Facebook pages, "Éco St-Zo" and "J 'achète St-Zo," to post the refurbished products. Residents can stop by the boutique on Fridays from 10 a.m. to 2 p.m. to get a better look at the items. All profits will go toward funding the project.
