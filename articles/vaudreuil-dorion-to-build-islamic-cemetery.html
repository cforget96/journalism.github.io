Vaudreuil-Dorion to build Islamic cemetery

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Jun 8, 2022

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion has approved plans to build a 4.5-acre Islamic cemetery south of Highway 20 on Saint Antoine Row, near the existing Catholic cemetery.

City officials say the project has been on file for four years, but plans to build the cemetery were stymied because the area was zoned as agricultural land. This changed after Vaudreuil-Soulanges MRC officials agreed to rezone the land because of its slope.

"The land cannot be cultivated," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "It never has been and never will be."

The Montreal Muslim community needs the space badly because of a severe lack of burial grounds to accommodate its growing population. At the moment, there are two cemeteries in the Montreal area, but they are both near capacity.

The project comes a month after the city approved zoning change that allows religious groups to occupy buildings in certain industrial and commercial zones.

The city also received a grant of nearly $30,000 from the provincial Ministry of Immigration, Francization and Integration to help accommodate the city's culturally and religiously diverse population.
