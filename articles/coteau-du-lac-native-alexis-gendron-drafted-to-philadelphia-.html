Coteau-du-Lac native Alexis Gendron drafted to Philadelphia Flyers

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 20, 2022

After weeks of anticipation, Alexis Gendron finally lived his dream of being drafted into the NHL on July 8. The 18-year-old from Coteau-du-Lac was taken by the Philadelphia Flyers 220th overall in the seventh.

Gendron just came off a strong season with the Blainville-Boisbriand Armada of the Quebec Major Junior Hockey league. He scored 30 goals in 66 games, good for 30th in scoring in a league usually dominated by players three years his senior.

Despite his intensity and scoring touch, Gendron's name was on the board until the very last round of the draft. Only about 5 per cent of players drafted in the seventh round will play 100 games in the NHL, which means the road to the NHL will be especially challenging for Gendron.

But there is always a chance that Philadelphia found a diamond in the rough in the 5-foot-9, 175-pound forward. For example, Dallas Stars forward Joe Pavelski, considered to be one of the best American-born players ever, was taken 205th overall in 2003 and is still an elite NHL talent at age 38.

Another example of an overlooked prospect is current Montreal Canadiens coach Martin St. Louis, who was inducted into the Hockey Hall of Fame in 2018 despite never being drafted at all. Before the 2022 draft began, St. Louis took to the Bell Centre stage to give some words of encouragement to the anxious prospects.

"Understand that [being drafted ] is just the beginning. It's probably the easy part," he said before a crowd of fans, prospects and their families. "Just keep pushing. You match work ethic with will, you can accomplish anything."

Gendron attended the Flyers' development camp from July 11-13 and will return to Philadelphia for the team's rookie camp in September.
