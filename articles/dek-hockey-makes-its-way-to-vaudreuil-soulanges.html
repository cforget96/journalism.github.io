Dek Hockey makes its way to Vaudreuil-Soulanges

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 6, 2022

Saint-Lazare residents may have noticed two full-sized hockey rinks, complete with scoreboards, in the parking lot of the Sports Complex on des Loisirs Street.

The lot has been rented by Association de Hockey Balle du Suroit, a new outdoor ball hockey league, and the only one in the region.

In its first season, which began in May, the association already has about 650 players and 66 teams between the junior and adult branches of the league, according to league treasurer and administrator Mélanie Gibouleau.

"Although we are the first Dek Hockey league in the region, there are many leagues throughout Quebec," Gibouleau said. "The sport has taken off in recent years, and we expect to continue with a fall season for adults."

What separates Dek Hockey from regular Ball Hockey is the surface. While Ball Hockey can be played on just about any surface, including concrete or gym floor, Dek Hockey uses special polyethylene shock-absorbent tiles. The tiles feature a grid design that allows dirt or water to pass through, meaning the surface can be used outdoors with relative ease.

Games take place on evenings every day of the week, though the junior league also plays on Saturday mornings. But you don't have to play in the league to use the rink. On gorendezvous.com/ahbds, the ice surfaces can be rented by the public for up to 12 hours at a time depending on availability.
