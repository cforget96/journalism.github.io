Border restrictions created a business boom for Hudson-Oka ferry

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 30, 2022

The Canada-US border has been laden with various restrictions for the better part of two years, making it especially difficult to cross by land. To avoid the hassle, many Quebecers have taken to "staycations," traveling across the province out of convenience and necessity.

As a result of the local travel, the Hudson-Oka ferry has seen an uptick in clients in recent seasons.

"We've definitely noticed a difference over the past two ferry seasons," said Claude Desjardins, the owner of the ferry. "Because borders were closed, people took the opportunity to explore the North Shore."

Desjardins declined to share figures that reflect the increase of traffic for the ferry, but he stated it was common for each ferry leaving the Hudson side to be full from the morning to the afternoon, while the Oka side was usually full in the evening.

"Most cyclists and pedestrians come from Hudson and go to Oka," he said. "The Oka dock is right in the heart of the village, so passengers take the ferry to walk around, go into shops, and eat at the waterside restaurants."

Desjardins expects the ferry to open in late April, though it could happen earlier depending on the weather.

"Last season we opened on April 12, which was quite early. We closed on November 19, so the ferry was open a full eight months" he said.

As of April 1, fully vaccinated travelers will no longer need to provide a negative COVID-19 test to re-enter Canada. With this change, it will be interesting to see if Quebecers flock into the States for a change of scenery, or if the pandemic has made them fall in love with their own back yard.
