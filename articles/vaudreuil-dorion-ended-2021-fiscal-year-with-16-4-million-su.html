Vaudreuil-Dorion ended 2021 fiscal year with $16.4 million surplus

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Aug 17, 2021

At the Vaudreuil-Dorion town hall on August 8, the town announced its financial report for the 2021 fiscal year. With operating revenues of $101.9 million and operating expenses of $85.5 million, the city ended the year with a $16.4 million surplus.

The majority of the surplus comes from property transfer taxes, which generated $19.8 million in revenue - $13.9 million more than the town had forecasted. Deputy General Manager and Treasurer Marco Pilon said that the spike came from the sale of two industrial buildings: one at 401 Marie Curie road and one in the Joseph Carrier industrial park.

Pilon also explained that $10 million -- 61 per cent of the $16.4 million surplus - has already been allocated to the municipal hub project to mitigate its future tax impact. But residents will have to wait until December to learn exactly how taxpayers will be affected in 2023.

In 2020, the Government of Quebec granted $3,410,000 in financial assistance to the city to help with the issues caused by the COVID-19 pandemic. Part of the sum was used in 2021 to lighten the tax burden of homeowners, to absorb income losses caused by canceled activities and to take proper sanity measures to adequately protect citizens and city employees.

Overall, the city decreased long-term debt by $4.3 million between December 31, 2020 and 2021. It now stands at $189.3 million, which Mayor Guy Pilon believes is very acceptable for a developing city like Vaudreuil-Dorion.

"With these results, the city is in a good position to pursue its development and continue to offer high-quality services to its citizens while maintaining an extremely competitive tax rate, both in the residential and commercial sectors," said Pilon.
