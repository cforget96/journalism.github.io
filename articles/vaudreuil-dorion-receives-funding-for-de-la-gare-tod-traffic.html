Vaudreuil-Dorion receives funding for de la Gare TOD traffic study

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 29, 2021

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion was recently selected as one of only 10 cities to receive up to $100,000 in funding from the Montreal Metropolitan Community (MMC). The financial aid will be used to conduct a study to improve traffic circulation using the TOD (Transit-Oriented Development) concept.

The TOD method encourages high-density development, mixing residences and businesses, to encourage pedestrians, cyclists, and public transportation commuters.

With upcoming development plans between de la Gare boulevard and Cité des Jeunes boulevard including the Vaudreuil-Soulanges hospital, a municipal library, and a new town hall, the TOD approach will play an essential role in managing an increased volume of traffic.

In a kick-off meeting last week, city officials met with consultants to establish a timeline.

The study will include an analysis of current traffic trends in existing developments and how they may be affected by future developments, explained Vaudreuil-Dorion communications director France Lavoie in a written statement to The 1019 Report.

"We will also evaluate the possibility of implementing an electric shuttle bus that will link the Vaudreuil train station with the future hospital site," Lavoie added.

The Vaudreuil-Soulanges hospital will "transform the landscape of Vaudreuil-Dorion over the next few years," according to the city's website. The hospital is slated to open in 2026.
