Vaudreuil Dorion Ecoproject awards

By Christopher Forget · Aug 3, 2022

Vaudreuil-Dorion awards $5000 grants to three local ecoprojects

As part of their Ecoprojects Community Fund, the city of Vaudreuil-Dorion awarded three grants of $5000 to projects that promote sustainable development in the community.

The projects eligible for financial assistance are the Garden of the Future at the Belles-Rives Adult Education Center, Electric Bicycle repair at Zèbre Rouge and L 'Atelier Paysan bike stop.

As part of the Garden of the Future at the Belles-Rives Center, students in the school's socio-professional integration program will be responsible for the complete food cycle of the garden, from planting to cooking. The project will teach students good environmental, consumption and culinary habits, in addition to developing their skills related to jobs in the horticultural, agricultural and culinary fields.

Electric bicycles are becoming increasingly popular, and the Zèbre Rouge project will promote the mode of active and eco-friendly transportation by restoring and reselling used e-bikes to the public. The grant will help train staff in the mechanics of electric bicycles in order to offer a repair service to residents.

Lastly, L'Atelier Paysan, an urban farm in partnership with IGA Famille Viau, plans to set up a cycling stop at 785 route de Lotbinière to promote physical activity and the discovery of urban agriculture. This stop will be used by volunteers and apprentice gardeners from L'Atelier Paysan, but also by the general public during visits to the farm and by cyclists.

These three organizations responded to the city 's open call last spring, which targeted citizens, non-profit organizations and public institutions wishing to carry out an environmental project in Vaudreuil-Dorion area. All projects will be implemented by the end of 2022.

"Organizations have submitted projects to us that each contribute in their own way to protecting the environment," said Vaudreuil-Dorion mayor Guy Pilon. "We are happy to participate in the implementation of these great initiatives through the Ecoprojects Community Fund."

The Ecoprojects Community Fund is part of the 2020-2025 "Habité par ma ville" strategic plan, which promotes citizen participation in the city's environmental policy. It aims to encourage initiatives that highlight the environment and its protection, including water resources, urban agriculture, waste, adaptation to climate change and biodiversity.
