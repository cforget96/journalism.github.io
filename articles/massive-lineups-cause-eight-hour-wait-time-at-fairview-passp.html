Massive lineups cause eight-hour wait time at Fairview passport office

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Undated

After two years of restrictions, many Montrealers are eager to travel abroad again, but the number of travelers is causing huge lineups at passport offices around the city. At the office inside of Fairview Pointe Claire, the lineups have forced people to arrive over four hours before the offices open, with many getting sent home despite their efforts.

Mohand Ameur, Beaubien

"I travel in July, so Service Canada recommended I come here. I wasn't able to get my passport the first time, so I came earlier this time. I arrived here at four in the morning and am still waiting almost eight hours later."

Carlos Ruiz, Brossard

"This is the first time I come to the Fairview location. I tried calling Service Canada to get more information because policies seem to change every day. I still haven't been able to get in touch with them."

James McNair, Kirkland

"I got here around 8:45 on Friday, which was much too late because people were already being sent home. I woke up around 3:30 in the morning today to make sure I get my passport."

Robert Porcheron, LaSalle

"This is my second time here. I was sent home after waiting in line for almost two hours, which was really frustrating. I made sure to get here earlier this time."

Elyssa Yielle, Brossard

"I arrived at Fairview at 5 this morning. I preferred to come here than downtown because there's parking at the mall. I originally filed for my children's passports in April, but it took over a month for them to tell me that there were some missing documents."

Genviève Riendeau, Beauharnois

"I tried to go to the office downtown, but I heard this one was shorter. Even still, I got here at 5:15. I want to make sure everything is settled so I can enjoy my trip."

Adejah Edouarzin, Roxboro

"We arrived at 4:50 this morning. I came earlier to ask the security guard when I should get here if I wanted to be seen. My mother told me all year that I should renew my passport, but I kept putting it off. I'm leaving in July."

Joanna Douillard, Pointe Claire

"The estimated wait time is two hours, which is not true. I arrived at quarter to 5. This is my third time here and every time I come a bit earlier. I guess third time's the charm."
