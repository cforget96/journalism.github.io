Festival des Couleurs returns to Rigaud to celebrate the fall

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 5, 2022

In 1997, a group of artisans and merchant worked together to create the Festival des Couleurs, a festival designed to celebrate the fall season, to entertain local families, and to put a spotlight on local items and produce.

25 years later, it has grown into one of the largest festivals in the 1019 region. In 2019, nearly 50,000 visitors attended the Festival des Couleurs in Rigaud. People from around the region and beyond come to enjoy the fall foliage around the mountain.

"The fall is when Rigaud offers its best colours, and the mountain really makes it unique in the region," said Christiane Lévesque, who has been the festival's Director General since 2015.

The festival offers a number of activities and performances to all age groups, including street performances, music shows, and more. During the day, the Marché des Saveurs features various specialty foods from local producers, while the Espace Artisans showcases local artisanal products.

The festival had grown every year until the COVID-19 pandemic hit. Planning festivals has become increasingly challenging in the years since.

"We came back last year, which was challenging because of the necessary precautions and restrictions that were in place at the time," Lévesque said.

Even with the restrictions, the festival welcomed about 15,000 visitors. That number is smaller because the post-pandemic festivals are limited to one site: Chartier-de Lotbinière park. In years prior to the pandemic, the Rigaud Ski Centre and Arbraska were also used as festival sites, but since they are private businesses, they no longer qualify as festival sites.

"The pandemic really made us re-evaluate our capacity," Lévesque said. "These days, there's a labor shortage, and it's harder to get materials, so we have to plan accordingly."

Despite some of the issues the festival has had to face, Festival des Couleurs is ready to welcome all visitors and share the variety of quality activities they have planned. Tickets are available for $5 per day at festivaldescouleurs.com, which goes toward the continued development of the festival.
