Vaudreuil-Dorion launches environmental policy survey for 2023-2027

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 20, 2022

With Vaudreuil-Dorion 's first environmental policy term coming to an end, the city wants to hear from its citizens to update the plan for the 2023-2027 term. The city launched a public survey on July 8 to ensure the new policy reflects the public's concerns about the environment.

Vaudreuil-Dorion's first environmental policy plan began in 2018 with the goal of implementing principles to manage the impact of municipal activities on the environment and sustainable development. In the ensuing years, the city took a number of steps to make Vaudreuil-Dorion a greener municipality by acquiring and preserving Harwood Nature Park, planting some 600 trees annually in public spaces and offering environment-related grants to citizens.

"The environment is at the heart of the city 's administration," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "We are doing a lot of work in this area and it is important that we continue to do so."

The survey for the 2023-2027 term includes 17 questions, including how the municipal government can help residents become more environmentally conscious and which environmental actions should be prioritized by the city.

Residents will also have the opportunity to express their opinion on the subject during Fêtes de quartier events, which take place every Wednesday evening in a different city park between 6 and 9 until August 24. The next event will take place on July 20 at Papillon-Bleu elementary school on des Loisirs St.

Vaudreuil-Dorion residents can fill out the survey until August 28 on the environmental policy page at ville.vaudreuil-dorion.qc.ca.
