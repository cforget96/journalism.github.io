Residential tax bills in Vaudreuil-Dorion to go up about $70 for homeowners

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Dec 22, 2021

The owner of average home in Vaudreuil-Dorion will pay just over 4 per cent more in municipal taxes next year, according to the city's $92-million budget approved earlier this month.

The increase represents a jump of about $70, bringing the 2022 [residential]{.mark} tax bill for a single-family home valued at $419,000 to $2,143.

"I know that citizens may be surprised by this increase, but we made it clear last year that the 2021 increase would be added to the 2022 rate. Despite this increase, we still have one of the lowest tax rates in the region," explained Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon.

According to the city's new budget, which will see a 6.5-per-cent increase in spending next year, the residential tax rate will be 51.16 cents per $100 of valuation. The rate for 2022 was dropped compared to the 2021 rate mostly in reaction to an overall hike in property valuations. For example, the value of an average single-family home in Vaudreuil-Dorion increased about $92,000 in the past year, representing a 28.2-per-cent jump compared to values in the previous year.

[The water tax rate will be $275 in 2022, which is the same as last year. However, due to upgrades to water and sewage treatment facilities, the city has revised the rate to reflect the true cost of using the resource. The rate is $275 for a residence served by water and sewage, but residences served only by water, for example, will be reduced to $165.]{.mark}

The average valuation of a condo in the municipality also increased in the last year, jumping 22.8 per cent, to $264,000, up from $215,000, according to the city's new triennial assessment roll, which comes into effect on Jan. 1. Since the increase in value of an average condo is less significant than the jump seen in the values of single-family homes, the owners of average condos will see a slightly smaller basic tax increase.

Overall, the city's budget for 2022 will see spending increase to $92,152,346, a hike of 6.5 per cent. More than half of the increase in expenses comes from a jump in the cost of the municipal payroll and social benefits package for municipal employees and police services. In total, these two elements constitute $4.2 million of the budget.

Vaudreuil-Dorion will be paying $6.3 million for Sûreté du Québec services in 2022, an increase of $1.9 million compared to this year.

Pilon said the city is contesting the way the policing charges are assessed. The legal challenge to the fee structured administered by the MRC of Vaudreuil-Soulanges could mean a difference of about $2 million for the city, he added.
