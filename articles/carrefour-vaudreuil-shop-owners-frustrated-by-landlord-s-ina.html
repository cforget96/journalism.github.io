Carrefour Vaudreuil shop owners frustrated by landlord's inaction

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Undated

As local shoppers continue to speculate which stores will replace the IGA, SAQ, and National Bank, the stakes are much higher for the tenants of Carrefour Vaudreuil. Many store owners believe their landlord has mismanaged the mall, which they believe will have negative consequences on the future of their businesses.

"People are really fed up," said Christine Robert, owner of Global Pet Foods and a Carrefour Vaudreuil tenant since 2007. "It's a stressful situation because you know nothing will be done in the short term, so you hope things will be fixed in the medium term."

Rumours have circulated about an Adonis or Giant Tiger moving into the former IGA location, but the space is in need of serious repair before anyone new can move in. Many stores have leaks in their roofs, and the owner of the plaza, Ventrum Inc., has not addressed this issue with a permanent solution.

"They've patched up my leaks three times and it still isn't fixed," said Claudia Wazen, the owner of Chaussures Panda and a tenant for nearly 12 years. "The doors are old and snow seeps in during the winter. The building hasn't been properly renovated since it was first built."

While the roof obviously needs to be redone, Robert noted that the most pressing repair needs to happen underground.

"My understanding is that everything underneath [the mall ] is not up to code. There's so much to be done on the sewers that there's no way another grocery store will replace the IGA until it's repaired," she said.

Ventrum was not available to comment on the state of the building, nor on who the new tenants for the SAQ, IGA, or National Bank may be.

"The only way I could get them to speak to me is if I were to stop paying rent," said Giant bike shop owner Tim Stocker about Ventrum. Having opened the franchise in 2017, Stocker is considering moving elsewhere when his lease is up.

"Rent has stayed the same, even though some of the biggest stores moving away has meant we've all lost business," Stocker added.

Despite the losses, additional lease costs have gone up each year. Ventrum has told shop owners that the hikes account for the small repairs and rising city taxes. However, after losing its biggest shops this past year, tenants were surprised to see that the additional lease costs were significantly lower than in years prior. Robert suspects that this was done to keep its current tenants around.

"A few of our leases are up next year, and Ventrum is probably scared that more people are going to leave," she said.

"I'm disappointed that a mall in such a prominent part of the city is deteriorating. [Ventrum ] hasn't kept it up to date," said Mayor Guy Pilon, a frequent shopper at Carrefour Vaudreuil. He noted that projects are on the way, but would not provide details about the projects nor when they could begin.

The state of the mall puts shop owners in a difficult situation. Many have had to restrict hours and let staff go over the pandemic, and a lack of communication between the owners and Ventrum has only made matters worse.

"Some people are afraid that nothing is going to happen and the place will be abandoned. But it's not my wish to leave; I'd rather stay," said Robert. She has spoken to other tenants about taking action against Ventrum, but it would require time and energy that they do not have at this particularly trying time.

Despite the uncertainty, Robert told The 1019 Report that her main priority is to serve her clientele.

"We work so hard to provide a good service because a lot of competition has developed over the past few years. I want people to know that if they want to support local, we do need and appreciate their business."
