Vaccination slowdown allows CISSSMO to open mobile clinics in lower-vaccinated areas

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 16, 2022

After more than a year of vaccinations, 81 percent of the Vaudreuil-Soulanges population has been adequately vaccinated, with 47 per cent of the population also having received the booster shot. At this stage of the pandemic, most people who plan on getting vaccinated have had the opportunity to do so and, as a result, vaccination clinics are experiencing a slowdown.

Since March 2021, the majority of vaccinations in the 1019 region have been at Centre Multisports in Vaudreuil-Dorion, where the CISSS of Montérégie-Ouest will continue to operate at a reduced capacity. Last December, the organization renewed their lease at the Centre with the city until May 2022, with the possibility of renewing the lease should the need arise.

"We are currently waiting for directions from the Ministry of Health and Social Services for the continuation of the vaccination activities," said CISSSMO Director of Communications Catherine Brousseau on whether they will require such a large space in the region moving forward.

The slowdown means there is additional manpower capacity, which has allowed CISSSMO to deploy mobile clinics in areas where vaccination rates are lower. The organization will set up temporary vaccination centres during the month of March in Sainte-Marthe, Hemmingford and Huntingdon. All clinics will serve the public with or without appointments.

Here are the locations of the three clinics along with their dates of operation:

Hemmingford - Recreation Centre, 550 Goyette Avenue

March 22 and 29, 1 p.m. to 7 p.m.

Huntingdon - Royal Canadian Legion, 8 Fairview Road

March 16, 22, 23, 29 and 30, 1 p.m. to 7 p.m.

Sainte-Marthe - Social and Sports Centre, 776 des Loisirs Street

March 17, 24 and 31, 1 p.m. to 7 p.m.
