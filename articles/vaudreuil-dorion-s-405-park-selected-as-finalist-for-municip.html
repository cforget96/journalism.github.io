Vaudreuil-Dorion's 405 park selected as finalist for Municipal Merit award

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 2, 2022

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion announced last week that The 405 project is a finalist for the Municipal Merit award from the Union of Municipalities of Québec (UMQ) in the COVID-19 category, which was created to celebrate the perseverance of municipalities during the pandemic.

Twenty-six projects were selected from among the 82 submitted. They were evaluated for the innovative nature of the project in its approach and results, the project 's potential for transferability or adaptability, the project 's impact on the community, the level of optimization and mobilization of resources, and the impact on the fight against climate change.

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion launched the 405 urban park in the summer of 2021 with the goal of breaking isolation and bringing hope to citizens by creating a destination where they could recharge their batteries, have fun and spend time with each other while respecting COVID health measures. The 405 was an immediate success with residents of all ages, so much so that the summer program was extended until October.

"Since March 2020, our employees have shown a great deal of adaptability and creativity, as evidenced by The 405, which has been a huge success with our citizens. We 're only talking about a few months between the project 's conception and the park 's opening to the public," said Mayor Guy Pilon of the various teams who worked on the project. Bravo! I am proud that this project has been recognized by our peers at the UMQ."

The 405 will be presented at the Pavillon of Quebec municipal innovation during the 100th edition of the UMQ convention, which will be held on May 12 and 13 at the Centre des Congrès in Québec City.
