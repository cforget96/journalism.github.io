Vaudreuil-Dorion launches survey for Harwood nature park

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Nov 24, 2021

After purchasing the 20-hectare green space formerly known as Harwood golf course for $2.3 million last spring, the city of Vaudreuil-Dorion wants to know which services residents would like to see in the Harwood nature park located off route 342 just past the Hudson-Oka ferry terminal.

The survey, which is available in English and French on the city's website under "News," will be available until December 6.

The priority is to conserve the green space, but the town also wants to create an inviting and accessible place for residents by designating areas of the park for activities and renting out the clubhouse, which includes a kitchen, for private events.

"We already have plans for the next few months, including the development of the dog park and winter activities like cross-country skiing. However, we want to take the time to think about a long-term offer, and to do this, we need to hear from the users of our facilities," said Vaudreuil-Dorion mayor Guy Pilon about the survey launch.

The survey comes as part of the city's 5-year plan, an important part of which includes encouraging community wellness and engagement.

The Harwood nature park, which is already accessible to the public, is one of several green spaces the city has acquired in recent years. With the rapid rise in population in the city over the past two decades, the parks are evidence of a shifting attitude in land development in the region.

"One thing is certain; no matter what services are proposed, this space will remain a nature park," Pilon said.
