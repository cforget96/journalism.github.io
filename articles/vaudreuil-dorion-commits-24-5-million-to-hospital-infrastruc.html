Vaudreuil-Dorion commits $24.5 million to hospital infrastructure

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · May 11, 2022

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion has committed to spend $24.5 million between now and 2026 to upgrade water and road infrastructure in preparation for the opening of the planned Vaudreuil-Soulanges Hospital.

The sum will finance two projects to accommodate the hospital construction project that is starting this summer. About $17 million will go towards building a new water reservoir near the hospital site on Cité des Jeunes Blvd. near the junction of Highway 40 and Highway 30[, Mayor Guy Pilon explained during last week's town hall.]{.mark} The reservoir will service the hospital, and replace the existing Dorion reservoir, which is nearing the end of its life, according to city officials.

The project will also include a number of upgrades to the city's water infrastructure, which will replace aging water mains, [city officials said]{.mark}. The city has had problems with breakdowns, including a major water main break in March 2022 that forced a boil water advisory in the area. [The city expects to begin work on the project by 2024.]{.mark}

Another $7.5 million will be dedicated to road infrastructure on Cité des Jeunes and De la Gare boulevards, as well as the redevelopment of the ramp at Exit 2 ramp along the eastbound Highway 30[, officials said.]{.mark}

The city will widen the Highway 30 exit ramp to improve traffic flow. Improving circulation has been a concern for Vaudreuil-Dorion and other neighbouring municipalities in and around the hospital site, which currently sees traffic jams during rush-hour periods. The volume of vehicles in the area is expected to increase with the opening of the hospital in 2026. Improving traffic flow has been deemed a necessity to allow ambulances easy access to the site at all times.

The work on Cité des Jeunes and De la Gare boulevards will also be required as the city installs water supply infrastructure to connect to the planned hospital water reservoir. This work will begin [in the summer]{.mark} and is expected to be completed by June 2023.

Construction of the new hospital, which has a price tag of $1.7 billion, is the largest public infrastructure project ever under taken in the Vaudreuil- Soulanges region. It will have 404 beds and employ about 3,000. It will also completely revamp the area around the site, according to hospital officials.

Earlier this year, the Vaudreuil-Soulanges Hospital project was ranked the 43rd largest public infrastructure project in the country and the sixth-largest health-care construction project currently in Canada.

Construction of the three pavilions that will make up the hospital will begin this summer. The facility is slated to open before the end of 2026.
