Vaudreuil-Dorion to offer dog owners funding for microchips

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 15, 2021

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion has allocated $14,500 of funding toward helping residents pay for their to be dogs microchipped.

The announcement comes after a bylaw that required all dogs older than 3 months to be permanently identified with the microchip as of June 30, 2021. Dogowners are eligible for a $10 rebate from the city, meaning the aid will be limited to the first 1450 applicants.

The financial assistance will be made available retroactively from when the bylaw came into effect on May 20, 2020 up until June 30.

The use of microchips has become popular among pet advocates as a way to identify animals who get lost. The city estimates the cost of microchipping a dog to be between $45 and $100, a procedure that can be done by a veterinarian and some dog control services, like the Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Animals, including the SPCA Ouest in Vaudreuil-Dorion.

Dog owners who refuse to comply with the bylaw risk being fined between $200 and $1,000 for a first offence, and $400 and $2,000 for subsequent offences.

The funding application form is available on the city's website under the "Animals" section.
