What's happening to Carrefour Vaudreuil?

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 7, 2021

After losing three of its biggest tenants to the newer, trendier Avenue Loisirs commercial development next to the roundabout south of Highway 40, it's unclear what the Carrefour Vaudreuil shopping plaza will do next.

IGA Extra Famille Déziel, the SAQ and the National Bank all moved to new locations in the Avenue Loisirs development. Two of the locations have already been inaugurated, with the National Bank branch set to open later this year.

These openings will surely move traffic away from the Carrefour Vaudreuil strip mall facing St. Charles Avenue, at least for the time being. The owner of the plaza, Ventrum Inc., declined to comment on the changes.

"The landowners never renewed or updated the complex," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon . "I can only think that the stores moved to the new commercial centre because it's a 'hot' spot."

As part of Harden's Les Avenues Vaudreuil development project, the new commercial development offers tenants a fresh look next to new neighbours. The development is already home to stores and restaurants like Starbucks, Sail and La Belle & La Boeuf. There is still room for one another building, which will only add to the area's buzz.

"Everyone is really enjoying the location," said Daniel Normand, the general manager at IGA Extra Famille Déziel. "It's a brand new store, and clients have only had good things to say about it."

Despite the big changes, the remaining tenants at the plaza along St. Charles are not concerned about losing business.

"We became an essential service over the pandemic," said Abdel Rougui, assistant manager at the Vidéotron branch in Carrefour Vaudreuil. "People need to come and see us here, whether they like it or not."

Ventrum has not yet announced who will be replacing the IGA, the SAQ and the National Bank in their old locations.
