Construction of the Vaudreuil-Dorion Municipal Hub to begin this fall

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 5, 2022

Since 1977, Vaudreuil-Dorion has been renting its city hall at 2555 Dutrisac Rd., and for decades has been planning to move to a new facility. This fall, the town will finally begin construction for a new town hall, and much more.

On September 19, the city announced that is has awarded the construction contract for phase 1 of a new Municipal Hub, which Vaudreuil-Dorion expects will become the civic and cultural centre of the town. The project will be located on Émile Bouchard Rd., behind the Multisports centre, and will bring together the town hall, a new library, and a public square. The town hall and the library will be united inside a single building.

Groupe Geyser de Laval was awarded a contract of $67.2 million for the project, which is in line with the city's 2022 estimates. A portion of the taxes, approximately $6 million, will be reimbursed by the government. A grant of over $4.8 million has also been announced by the governments of Quebec and Canada for the construction of the new library.

In June 2021, the city rejected the only bid it received for the construction of the Municipal Hub, and ultimately put the project on hold due to market overheating. Only a year later, the city decided it would start acting despite the higher price tag.

"Admittedly, the price is higher than our 2020 estimates, but these were made before the price spike. The market is completely different and we cannot afford to wait forever," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "As I have mentioned before, our city is in excellent financial health and the many investments to come, including the hospital, will provide us with significant income. We will respect our commitment to limit the impact on our citizens' tax bills."

In the long term, the town plans to add an aquatic complex to the Municipal Hub, but this will only come in a later phase of the project depending on the availability of government subsidy programs. Construction work on the municipal hub will begin this fall and should be completed by the end of 2024.
