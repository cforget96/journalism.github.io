Ministry of Transport installs photo radar on westbound side of Ile-au-Tourtes bridge

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 7, 2022

In the five months since The 1019 Report last conducted a study on photo radar infractions in the region, the mobile radar along highway 40 in Vaudreuil-Dorion has recorded 8034 infractions, representing nearly $2.5 million in fines since March 31.

Now, the Ministry of Transport announced that another photo radar will be installed on the westbound side of the Ile-aux-Tourtes bridge near the Senneville exit, meaning that there will be mobile radars on either side of the bridge. The westbound photo radar will remain there until July 2023, but MTQ spokesman Louis-André Bertrand says it could stay there longer.

"There will be mobile photo radar surveillance around the site until the bridge is rebuilt," he said. "It is too early at this time to determine the exact timeline for completion of the bridge, however."

Numbers published by the Quebec Ministry of Justice recorded that photo radars handed out $31.5 million in fines province-wide over the first seven months of 2022, suggesting that it will be a record year in photo radar fines.

Like all photo radar stops, demerit points will not be added for motorists caught by the new radar in Senneville, but the fines are doubled when the violation is committed in a road work zone.

Nearly 87,000 vehicles take the Ile-aux-Tourtes bridge every day, approximately 10 per cent of which are trucks. Though Bertrand could not determine exactly when construction will be completed, but the MTQ projects the new bridge should be open by 2027.
