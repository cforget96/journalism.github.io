RMC releases wetlands survey to public

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Sep 29, 2021

The Vaudreuil-Soulanges Regional Municipal Council shared a survey regarding the future its wetlands earlier this month. The aim of the survey is "to collect [the ] concerns, suggestions, and expectations" of both citizens and project collaborators in the region.

The survey comes as the council works to finalize its new plan regarding the wetlands. The plan must be submitted to the Minister of the Environment and Climate Change by June 16, 2022.

"The future of our wetlands are important, and citizen input is essential to the planning process," RMC Prefect Patrick Bousez told The 1019 Report.

The survey meets the provincial government's demands, which requires the RMC to consult the public as part of its planning.

As consultations about the wetlands continue, citizen-led environmental activism group MARE believes that the RMC should be doing more.

"Since the submission of our requests last May, we have not noticed any change on the part of the MRC concerning the protection of natural environments. Citizens are still very concerned about the loss of their forests and wetlands," says MARE spokesman David Pharand.

The survey will be available on the RMC website and Facebook page until October 5.
