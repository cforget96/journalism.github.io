Election race in St Michel district could result in only opposition in mayor Pilon's council

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 27, 2021

Only residents of one district in Vaudreuil-Dorion will be making their way to the polls in the Nov. 7 municipal election. And they will determine if a sole opposition voice will get a seat on council.

Only one candidate has stepped forward to give veteran St. Michel district councillor Gabriel Parent a run for the seat in the central ward of the largest municipality in the Vaudreuil-Soulanges region. That challenger is Denis Lecompte, who is making his first foray into municipal politics, running as an independent candidate.

If he wins, Lecompte would be the only person on council who is not part of Mayor Guy Pilon's Parti de l'Action de Vaudreuil-Dorion. All other 7 members of Pilon's party were acclaimed last month. Pilon himself was acclaimed to another term, his fifth since first being elected in 2005.

Lecompte hopes to prioritize planning for the rapid population growth in Vaudreuil-Dorion. He believes that future plans, like the Vaudreuil-Soulanges Hospital, and the possibility of another bridge or an REM commuter train station, will contribute to the municipality's growth.

Now retired, Lecompte aims to bring his 40 years of experience as an entrepreneur, consultant and civil engineer to office. He has also served on multiple boards of directors in the finance, culture, and health sectors. He currently sits on the board of directors for the Onze de la Gare apartment complex.

But unseating Parent will be no easy feat. First elected with Pilon in 2005, he is running on a strong record. He is particularly proud of the projects of his last mandate, including the 405 park and the improvement of safety around Ste. Madelaine and St. Michel elementary schools, all of which came to fruition despite the pandemic and the multiple floods in the area.

Prior to his work as a city councillor, Parent worked as the director of maintenance and repairs for the Montreal Housing Office and as director of operations for Domtar. Parent serves on a number of committees, including environment, finance, recreation and public security.
