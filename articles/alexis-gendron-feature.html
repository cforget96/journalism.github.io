Alexis Gendron feature

By Christopher Forget · Jun 22, 2022

Coteau-du-Lac native prepares for 2022 NHL Entry Draft

18-year-old Alexis Gendron hopes to hear his name called at the 2022 NHL Entry Draft in Montreal next month.

Raised in Coteau-du-Lac, Gendron grew up playing hockey in the Soulanges minor league system and was enrolled in the sports-études program at Chêne-Bleu high school in Ile Perrot before playing in the Quebec Major Junior Hockey League. He credits growing up in the region for much of his success.

"My first year at Chêne-Bleu, we won the provincial championship, which was a great experience," he said. "Most of my friends are from the region and they pushed me to be better."

Gendron was drafted by the Blainville-Boisbriand Armada in 2020 straight out of high school. He just came off of a strong season playing forward for the Armada, compiling 30 goals in 66 games. He is ranked 141st on the final edition of the NHL Central Scouting list, meaning he is projected to go in the fifth of seven rounds. Naturally, Gendron has been nervous in the weeks leading up to the draft, but he has developed strategies to prepare for the big day.

"I've been training a lot. Some teams have reached out to interview me. It's a bit nerve-wracking at first, but once the first question is out of the way, it's fine. The more I do it, the easier it becomes," Gendron said.

At 5-foot-10, Gendron is four inches shorter than the average NHLer. To make up for his lack of size, he says he has developed his defensive game and his competitiveness over the past season. He tries to model his game after a fellow undersized Quebecer, Anthony Beauvillier of the New York Islanders.

"He's a smaller guy who moves fast with a good shot. He's constantly in movement and puts a lot of pressure on the other team's defense. That's what I admire about his game," Gendron said.

Although he grew up in Coteau-du-Lac, Gendron was actually born in Olten, Switzerland. His father, Martin Gendron, suited up for the Washington Capitals and the Chicago Blackhawks before moving overseas to play pro hockey.

Gendron is also the nephew of NHL player agent Pat Brisson, who hails from Valleyfield. Brisson's son, Brendan, was selected in the first round of the 2020 NHL Draft by the Vegas Golden Knights.

Hockey talent clearly runs in the family, which Gendron sees as an asset.

"Growing up in and around the hockey world really shows you what it takes to make it. I think that will help me a lot in the future," he said.

The NHL Entry Draft will be held at the Bell Centre on July 7 and 8.
