Soulanges canal park to host bevy of events and activities in August

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Aug 3, 2022

On a calm evening, the western end of the Soulanges canal is one of the most breathtaking views in Vaudreuil-Soulanges. Looking out onto Saint-Francis at sunset is the picture of natural beauty, which makes it a great place to visit this summer.

The administrators of the canal want residents from all over the 1019 region to appreciate the beauty of the site, which is why it has put together another summer of programming for L'Embouchure pointe festive, an ephemeral park which will host a number of events ranging from food, live music, games and much more.

The Soulanges canal park will offer events every Friday, Saturday and Sunday until August 27 at both Thomas Monro park in Coteau-du-Lac and Louis Stanislas Pariseau park in Les Coteaux.

One of the biggest draws at L'Embouchure is its snack bar, which sells local wines and beers including Vignoble Pomone, Bourg des Cèdres, Brasserie Bois-Blanc and Microbrasserie Trois-Lacs.

Because l'Embouchure is located at the mouth of the canal, which is where it gets its name, it is the perfect place for water activities. During the day, the park is offers paddle board and kayak rentals. It is the only place in the region that offers lessons for windsurfing, surfing and electric surfing, making for a unique experience. The park also offers electric bikes with oversized wheels for rides along the canal.

Of course, l'Embouchure will host evenings of song and dance throughout the month of August. While most of the events are free, several live music evenings presented by Desjardins Vaudreuil-Soulanges require tickets. Ticket bundles are available at the park's website, www.lembouchure.ca.

Competition project taking shape

In September 2021, the Vaudreuil-Soulanges MRC held a press conference to announce their partnership with the Ministry of Transport to revitalize the Soulanges canal, which they believe has untapped potential as a local tourist destination.

Under the MTQ, parts of the canal had become decrepit, which the MRC plans to change. The park's mission is to develop, enhance and animate the Soulanges canal from the angles of heritage, environment, culture and sustainable development.

At the press conference, the MRC announced the winners of a competition for the group that had the best idea to revitalize the canal. The winners were ESCARGO collective, who proposed their project, Passagers. The vision includes multiple stops along the 23-kilometre canal, with each stop highlighting an aspect of the canal's natural, cultural, and historical significance. The project includes outdoor art installations, ice skating in the winter, and a revamped square around one of the canal's most recognizable landmarks, Le Petit Pouvoir.

Nearly a year later, Soulanges canal park director Marianne Sigouin-Lebel says progress has been made on the project.

"We are currently working with the winners of the competition and drafting the Cultural and Landscape Master Plan," said Sigouin-Lebel. "Delivery of the plan is scheduled for the end of the summer, which will guide the developments over the coming years in terms of infrastructure, integration of culture, signage and landscaping and ecological management."

Scuba diving reopens in Pointe-des-Cascades

Whether it's your first time putting on a scuba suit or you're a seasoned veteran, the Soulanges canal park is the place to get your feet wet this August. For a $5 access fee, any visitor can dive 20 feet and look for sunken artifacts at the newly-reopened scuba site in Pointe-des-Cascades.

Located at the heart of the locks of the Soulanges canal, the site was taken over by the Soulanges canal park from the MTQ in 2021. The site has been accredited by La Route Palmée, the network of Quebec dive sites that meets the safety, accessibility and quality standards. The site includes a equipment bench, a changing area, picnic areas, bathrooms and parking.

Recognized and subsidized by the Ministry of Education, the scuba site's mission is to bring together enthusiasts of the underwater environment, to ensure their safety, and to promote underwater activities such as scuba diving, underwater hockey and rugby, freediving and webbing.
