Saint-Zotique beach attracts visitors from all over in 40th year

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 6, 2022

Saint-Zotique beach is celebrating its 40th anniversary this summer, and visitors came out in droves for Saint-Jean weekend.

"We had reached our 4000-person capacity, so we had to turn away some visitors who hadn't purchased online tickets in advance," said Isabelle Dalcourt, the beach's Director. "We get more and more visitors each year, and this despite the limited capacity of the last two years because of COVID. We had nearly 100,000 visitors last year."

The beach officially opened on June 11, so it's too early to tell if the beach will draw even more visitors this season. But tourism helps local business according to Peter Papadopoulos, the owner of Souvlaki Saint-Zotique, a restaurant five minutes from the beach.

"It's always good in the summer, and the tourism definitely helps," said Papadopoulos. "The beach is really a hidden gem and I think more people are learning about it. It's like coming to Cuba without getting on a plane."

Papadopoulos was among the ones who stumbled upon the area and decided to stay. He came from NDG 13 years ago and has been in Saint-Zotique ever since, running the restaurant with his wife, Sarah.

"I figured the people of Saint-Zotique needed a Greek guy to feed them," he joked. "For the longest time it was predominately French-Canadian, but that's started to change in the last few years."

In fact, the combination of undeveloped land and beautiful scenery has made Saint-Zotique among the five fastest-growing cities in Quebec. According to data from Statistics Canada, the population has grown by 21.2 per cent in the last five years. The beach may have been a hidden gem a decade ago, but that may no longer be the case.

"It really is a great getaway, 30 minutes from the city. People are taking notice," Papadopoulos said.

The beach will host a number of activities throughout its 40th-anniversary season, including evenings of music, light shows, barbecues, and beach activities and beach yoga during the day.
