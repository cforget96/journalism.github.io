Vaudreuil-Dorion allots over $200 million to major projects in new five-year plan

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Jan 5, 2022

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion has announced the projects it hopes to complete in its new quinquennial plan, which spans from 2022 to 2026

"In 2022, we want citizens of all ages to have access to a variety of services in our city, at a low cost," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon.

The combined sum of these projects totals nearly $220 million, and three of them will cost about $45 million or more.

Municipal Hub ($51 million, 2022-23)

The most costly of these projects will be the Municipal Hub, which Vaudreuil-Dorion expects will become the civic and cultural centre of the town. The project will be located on Émile Bouchard Rd., behind the Multisports centre.

Once finished, the Hub will include access to the train station and bus terminal, a new city hall, a public square, a library, and an aquatic centre. The city has been renting the current city hall at 2555 Dutrisac Rd. since 1977 and has long been planning a move to a new facility.

The city's quinquennial plan implies that construction of the Hub will begin within the next two years despite announcing in June 2021 that, due to market overheating, plans for the library and city hall would be put on hold. The city rejected the only bid received for the construction of the library, city hall and a public square for this reason.

Well, treatment, reservoir and municipal services for hospital ($48.7 million, 2022-23-24)

In preparation for the Vaudreuil-Soulanges hospital, which is set to open its doors in 2026, the city will continue to put the necessary infrastructure in place over the coming years.

Recent developments include the demolition of the building located at 2145 Cité des Jeunes Blvd. in October, which will make the intersection with de la Petite-Rivière Rd. compatible with the construction of the hospital. A disused gas station on Cité des Jeunes Blvd. was also dismantled in September; a gas pump from the station was preserved for potential future use.

Aquatic complex ($44.2 M -- 2025-26)

Plans for the aquatic complex have been underway since fall 2018. The city submitted a grant application through the Financial Assistance Program for Recreational and Sports Infrastructures that could make them eligible for a $20 million dollar grant from both federal and provincial governments.

The city has also dedicated $12.5 million to improve its parks and green spaces.

"We are following our strategic planning roadmap to provide our citizens with a humane living environment and a variety of quality services. In 2022, we want to expand our grants for the renovation of heritage buildings, update our environmental policy and implement a citizen 's ecoproject program, plan and begin the development of Harwood Nature Park, plan programming for the 405 Park and review the sports fields in four of our parks," Pilon added.

Other projects in the quinquennial plan include road repairs to Chicoine Rd. and Lotbinière Rd. and the expansion of Cité des Jeunes Blvd. The city also plans to begin building a new fire station on Lotbinière Rd. for an estimated $15 million in 2022-2023.
