High gas prices putting West Islanders in a bind

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET 1510 West · Jul 12, 2022

Luc Martin, Vaudeuil-Dorion

"I'm constantly on the road for work, so I don't really have a choice but to fill up. It definitely hurts my wallet, though."

Kevin Locras, Pierrefonds

"I wish I could spend less, but I drive a pickup. I try to keep my tank full because it helps with fuel efficiency."

Sabrina Stevenson, Lachine

"I'm taking a train to Southern Ontario with a friend to avoid spending money on gas. I also try to carpool whenever I can."

Jeffrey Johnson, Dollard-des-Ormeaux

"I've been putting in regular gas instead of supreme to save some money, but I still need to get around."

Mike Amelotte, Dollard-des-Ormeaux

"I use the Gas Buddy app to find the best prices in the area. My wife and I both have fuel-efficient cars, so long-distance driving has been okay. We went to visit our son in Fredericton and paid less than I expected."

Élisée Regis, Sainte-Genviève

"I work as a personal shopper, so it's been hard. I'm always on the road and probably spend $200 a week on gas."

Steve Sawoch, Pointe-Claire

"I work in the West Island and my wife takes the train downtown, so we don't spend too much on gas. Many of my colleagues began carpooling when the gas price shot up, though."

Liam Quraeshi, Baie-d'Urfé

"I prefer to bike and take the bus whenever I can. Both my parents have electric vehicles, so thankfully the gas prices haven't bothered us too much."
