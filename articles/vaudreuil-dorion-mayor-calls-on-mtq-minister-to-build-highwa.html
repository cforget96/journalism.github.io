Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor calls on MTQ Minister to build highway 20 bypass

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Mar 30, 2022

After the MTQ announced its plan to widen highway 50 over a 96-kilometre distance, Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon is wondering when highway 20 will receive similar attention. In a letter to Minister of Transport François Bonnardel, Pilon made his plea to begin the construction of a Highway 20 bypass in the Harwood Blvd. urban sector.

The Mayor expressed his frustration with the decision to prioritize work on highway 50 over highway 20, and he had the figures to support his case. According to the Vaudreuil-Dorion's report, approximately 75 per cent of the trade between Quebec, Ontario, and the United States transits through Vaudreuil-Dorion.

"The government is withholding all of our arguments on the bypass to justify the widening of highway 50," said Pilon in his letter to Bonnardel. "They say that highway 50 is a privileged commercial link between Ontario and Quebec, but the Vaudreuil-Dorion region is THE link for trade and transit between Quebec and Ontario."

According to the city, 16,000 motorists use highway 50 on a daily basis compared to nearly 50,000 in the Harwood urban section of highway 20. Pilon noted that the already heavy traffic was exacerbated during the emergency closure of the Île-aux-Tourtes bridge in May 2021 when all of the area's traffic was rerouted through the Harwood sector.

In addition to this, Pilon added that during the Île-aux-Tourtes bridge closure, residents could not access the major Montreal hospitals because of the traffic. Without a bypass for highway 20, he says the current configuration is in a "precarious condition" in terms of safety.

Road safety was one of the reasons the Quebec government justified the highway 50 project. Pilon believes the MTQ should take action in Vaudreuil-Dorion for the same reason, especially because of its proximity to schools, pedestrian crosswalks, businesses and a retirement home.

"We are constantly one serious accident away from a tragedy," Pilon warned in his letter.
