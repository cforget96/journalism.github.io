Route de Lotbinière to undergo 12 million-dollar transformation

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Apr 13, 2022

The city of Vaudreuil-Dorion announced on April 4 that it will be taking out a 12 millon-dollar loan to revamp Route de Lotbinière, the stretch of road that connects Route Harwood in Dorion to Pointe-des-Cascades.

The loan will cover all costs for the project, which includes repaving the road and introducing sidewalks, bike lanes, and lighting to make the road more accessible to cyclists and pedestrians.

The road is already popular among cyclists for its waterfront view and its connection to the Soulanges canal, but the proposed installations will improve safety given that the speed limit on the route goes up to 90 kilometres per hour.

The work on Route de Lotbinière is one in a series of projects in the area the city intends to redevelop into a "second downtown," according to Vaudreuil-Dorion Director General Olivier Van Neste.

"There are studies underway at the moment to reroute highway 20 to go along the train tracks, which will make the entire Harwood area more accessible," Van Neste said. "We think there is a lot of potential for re-development along Harwood, Chicoine and de Lotbinière. Right now, you don't want to go out on your balcony or walk over to get a coffee on a road that 50,000 vehicles pass through every day, so we're hoping that rerouting the 20 will invite more foot traffic in the area."

There is no set date for when highway 20 will be rerouting away from the Harwood commercial area, but the series of projects in the area and Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy-Pilon's letter to Minister of Transportation François Bonnardel suggest that the city is preparing for the project to come to fruition in the coming years.

Van Neste said the de Lotbinière project will take between 12 and 18 months to finish, and that the city hopes to begin construction in the fall of 2022.
