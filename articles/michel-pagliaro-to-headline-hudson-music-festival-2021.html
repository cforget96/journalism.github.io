Michel Pagliaro to headline Hudson Music Festival 2021

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Undated

Famed Canadian rocker Michel Pagliaro might be in his 70s now, but he has not retired. He will be one of the lead performers at this year's festival.

Artistic director and co-founder of the Hudson Music Festival Blair Mackay announced that four live-music events will be taking place between July 30 and August 2.

"While certain COVID-19 regulations will still be in effect to ensure the safety and security of our audiences, it will still feel wonderful to be out with friends once again enjoying live music. We've brought together a group of artists to acknowledge our past and look ahead to brighter days," said Mackay.

Pagliaro will perform at 8pm on Saturday, July 31 at the Stephen F. Shaar Community Centre. Other performances include singer Karen Young and pianist Marianne Trudel performing the music of Joni Mitchell (8pm July 30 at the Alibi Barn), an evening of James Taylor by Denis Ducharme (8pm August 1 at Stephen F. Shaar), and Musica Ouest, a classical music quartet (7pm August 2 at St-Thomas Aquinas Church).

An icon of Canadian music, Pagliaro was nominated for a 1975 Juno Award as best male vocalist. He received a Governor General's Performing Arts Award in 2008, and his song "J'entends frapper" was inducted into the Canadian Songwriter's Hall of Fame in 2010. Although he records most of his music in French, he has released English material over his career.

The festival celebrates its 15-year anniversary this summer.
