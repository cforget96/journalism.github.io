Nez Rouge returns for 2021 holiday season after COVID break

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Dec 8, 2021

After COVID-19 stymied holiday gatherings in 2020, the Vaudreuil-Soulanges Nez Rouge service is back up and running this season. Last year, instead of chauffeuring festive partygoers, the organization ran an awareness campaign.

Nez Rouge will be based at the Pincourt Omnicentre and will be in operation during the evenings of Dec. 10, 11, 17, 18 and 23. They can be reached at (450) 424-4011.

Although the organization is happy to be offering the service again this holiday season, the pandemic has created new obstacles to consider.

"We have everything in place to ensure the safety of clients and volunteers, but we still need more volunteers to form teams. In order to be in accordance with public health measures, volunteers must have their vaccination passports," said Isabelle Corbeil, the general manager of Répit le Zéphyr. In addition to operating the seasonal Nez Rouge service, Zéphyr is a caregiving service that provides aid to families with one or more dependents diagnosed with an intellectual disability or autism disorder.

So far this campaign, which began on Nov. 27, 31 volunteers have travelled 188 km and raised $2160. This year, the city of Vaudreuil-Dorion donated $1000 to Zéphyr.
