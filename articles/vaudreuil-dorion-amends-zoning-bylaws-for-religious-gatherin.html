Vaudreuil-Dorion amends zoning bylaws for religious gatherings

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Mar 16, 2022

Religious communities will now be permitted to gather in buildings within Vaudreuil-Dorion's commercial zones following an amendment to a city bylaw on March 7. The city vowed in its 2020-2025 strategic plan to "make diversity a source of wealth," and these new regulations create a blueprint for the establishment of new places of worship where there previously were none.

The amendment comes after a controversy that for years has surrounded the Islamic Cultural Centre of Vaudreuil-Soulanges's operation out of 2400 Saint-Antoine St. in Dorion, the former location of Sunny's restaurant. A group of nearby residents opposed the building's sale to the Islamic Centre, but the sale ultimately went through. Following the sale, probing residents suspected that the space was being used as a mosque despite the area being an industrial zone.

In the fall of 2020, a group of 157 residents signed a petition opposing a decision by the city that would allow the opening of an Islamic house of worship on the site. Ultimately, the city cancelled the referendum. Over a year later, city officials hope the zoning change will bring stability to the situation.

"The events of the last few years in connection with the relocation of the Islamic Cultural Centre have made us realize that there is a problem with regard to places of worship," said Vaudreuil-Dorion mayor Guy Pilon. "That 's why we looked at the regulations to make sure that places were identified to accommodate these uses. We have had requests on a few occasions, which is quite legitimate, and it is important to have precise guidelines to supervise them. "

The city noted in a press release that it has undergone a major demographic transformation over the past twenty years. To account for this, Vaudreuil-Dorion is has obtained financial assistance from the Ministries of Immigration, Francization and Integration for projects that will "develop mechanisms to welcome, integrate and support all new residents" and "renew the community's sense of belonging and to develop a welcoming and inclusive community life."

The grant will fund 50% of the costs associated with the project, to a maximum of $27,500. The city anticipates that the project will extend well into 2022 so the policy can be adopted in the winter of 2023.

A face-to-face public consultation will be held on March 28 to allow citizens to ask questions about the new zoning bylaws. Details will be posted by the end of the week on the city 's website in the Public Meetings section.
