Cross-country skiing surges in 1019 region

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Feb 2, 2022

Across Vaudreuil-Soulanges, cross-country skiing has become a shockingly popular pastime over the COVID-19 pandemic.

For reference, the total number of visitors at the Les Forestiers nature park in St. Lazare jumped from 4833 in 2019-2020 to a whopping 15 049 in 2020-2021. The number of season passes also rose from 30 to 125 in the same years.

While it is too early to determine whether numbers have continued to increase in the 2021-2022 season, preliminary figures point to it being at least as popular this year as it was last year.

"We have sold 92 season passes this season, despite weather conditions that delayed the opening of the trails. Because of this, the cross-country ski trails have only been groomed and open since January 18," said Rachel Gauthier-Langlois, Event Coordinator for the town of St. Lazare.

On Ile Perrot, the Skieurs de l'Ile cross-country trails are experiencing a similar boom in popularity.

"In 2020-2021, we had 1257 subscriptions, which was up from 636 the previous season. The number of subscriptions used to hover around 600 in prior years, so this is a big jump," said Pierre Levac, director of Skieurs de l'Ile. Levac added that the organization counted over 1000 subscriptions for this season, with more to come as the season continues.

As the number of cross-country skiers climb, however, organizers will have to create more parking spaces. This is especially true for Skieurs de l'Ile, a network of trails which passes through all five municipalities of the island, as one of its entrances is located on Boulevard de l'Ile, one of Pincourt's central arteries.

"We are indeed seeing pressure on the availability of spaces on weekends, especially on Boul. de L 'île and at the Notre Dame de Fatima Centre in Notre Dame de l'Ile Perrot. The center had to solicit its clientele to use other accesses and parking lots nearby last winter. While these entrances are particularly popular, it should be noted that there are seven other access points to the trails, many of which offer a good supply of parking spaces," Levac added.

Vaudreuil-Dorion has also created new cross-country trails for its residence at Harwood nature park. Though much of the 200,000 square-metre park is deforested due to its previous use as a golf course, residents have a variety of winter activities to choose from. On weekends, rental equipment includes snowshoes, crampons, fatbikes, and, of course, cross-country equipment.

Other locations for cross-country ski in the 1019 region include:

Centre de la nature quatre saisons (160 Route 338 in Coteau du Lac)

Sentiers de l'escapade du Mont Rigaud (15 Boisé des Franciscaines Rd. in Rigaud)

St. Zotique Beach (105 8st Ave. in St. Zotique)

Whitlock Gold Club (128 Côte Saint-Charles in Hudson)

Pointe du Moulin Park (2500 Don Quichotte Blvd. in Notre Dame de l'Ile Perrot)
