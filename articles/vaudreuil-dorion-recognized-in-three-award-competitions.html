Vaudreuil-Dorion recognized in three award competitions

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · May 25, 2022

Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon announced during town hall last week that the municipality was in the running for three separate awards for the town's work over the last year.

The city was a finalist, along with Gatineau and Longueil, for the "COVID-19 Special Award" at the Union of Municipalities of Quebec conference. The city was nominated for The 405 project, which was designed to bring residents of all ages together in an outdoor setting over the pandemic.

Although Longueil took the prize, Vaudreuil-Dorion won the "Votre Coup de Coeur" award, which was given to the municipality that won the hearts of the participants at the conference.

The municipality has also been recognized internationally; Vaudreuil-Dorion was named to a list of 50 cities to begin a selection process by United Cities and Local Governments. The UCLG Cultural Award recognizes cities and individuals who have excelled in promoting cultural rights in the context of the COVID-19 crisis or who have promoted culture as an important part of the health care system.

The city was recognized by UCLG in 2016 when it won the Cultural Award for its Mozaïk Network, which is represented by the "Je Suis" sign at Maison-Valois park.

The Vaudreuil-Dorion communications team was also recognized by the Quebec Municipal Communications Association as a finalist for "plumes d'exellence" contest. The team was nominated for its work on surharwood.com, a website dedicated to information about the development of the Harwood-de Lotbinière sector of the municipality, which the city is making an effort to transform into a "second downtown," according to Mayor Pilon. The winner of the contest will be announced on June 2.
