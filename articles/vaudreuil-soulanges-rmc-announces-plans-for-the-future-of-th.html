Vaudreuil-Soulanges RMC announces plans for the future of the Soulanges Canal

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 15, 2021

The future of the Soulanges canal was unveiled at a press conference at Anchor Park in Pointe-des-Cascades on Monday afternoon.

Three announcements were made: the RMC's agreement with the Ministry of Transportation for joint management of the canal, the formation of the Soulanges canal park management Society, and the announcement of the winner of the competition for the cultural and landscape development of the Soulanges Canal Park.

While the agreement between the RMC and the MTQ stipulates that the MTQ will continue to govern the structural components of the canal, the RMC now has control of the grounds around the canal, which will allow them to revitalize the area. The Soulanges canal park management Society creates a designated body to oversee canal-related projects.

The winners of the competition, a team composed of the Collectif Escargo firm in collaboration with the Rousseau Lefebvre architectural firm and museologist and cultural project consultant Geneviève Murray, announced their proposal, Passagers. The vision includes multiple stops along the 23-kilometre canal, with each stop serving a different purpose. The projects in the proposal include outdoor art installations, ice skating, and a revamped square around one of the canal's most recognizable landmarks, Le Petit Pouvoir.

"Our first priority is to make the canal accessible to as many people as possible in the region," said RMC Prefect Patrick Bousez. "We want the area to be lively and to be a symbol of pride for the region."
