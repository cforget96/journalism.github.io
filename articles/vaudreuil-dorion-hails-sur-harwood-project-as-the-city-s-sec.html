Vaudreuil-Dorion hails Sur Harwood project as the city's "second downtown"

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jul 6, 2022

The face of Vaudreuil-Dorion has changed over the last two decades, but most of the development has come along the northern part of the city on highway 40. Now, the city is focusing on transforming the Harwood area into a pedestrian-friendly commercial boulevard.

The city has approved projects along Harwood Blvd. and streets adjacent to it, including Chicoine and de Lotbinière. Some examples are apartment buildings along Harwood and Chicoine, including on the site of the former Miss Dorion, a new fire station on De Lotbinière, and a new secondary school.

"We want to transform the Harwood area into a second downtown," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon at a recent city council meeting. "You may begin noticing changes by the end of 2022 or in 2023."

However, Olivier Van Neste, the city's General Manager, stressed that the complete transition will take some time.

"While many projects have already been accepted by the city, they will certainly not all be built at the same time," Van Neste said. "At some point, the city will need to upgrade some of the infrastructure to accommodate subsequent phases of development, as was already anticipated at the time of the last Harwood urbanization initiative in 2016."

The Sur Harwood website features several photo projections of what the area will look like once development is finished, complete with public courtyards, wide sidewalks with bike paths and new shops. But the website states that this will only become a reality in 2035.

In the meantime, the city has already completed projects along Harwood that needed revitalizing, such as a new mixed-use building on Chicoine which used to be an old bowling alley, the Vaudreuil-Soulanges RCM office and regional court on a previously vacant lot, and the Long & McQuade music store which used to be a liquidation furniture store. These newer buildings stand out next to the older shops, but the city is continually looking for new commercial and residential investors to breathe new life in the area.

Harwood revitalization hinges on rerouting Highway 20

While the city has already taken measures to reimagine the Harwood area, but the city's vision hinges on rerouting the Highway 20 away from Harwood to make it attractive and safe for pedestrians.

"The vision for the development of the Harwood area assumes that the Highway 20 bypass will be completed within the next few years. 50,000 vehicles use Harwood every day, and we are fully aware that this does not make it an optimal living environment," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Director General Olivier Van Neste.

The city has been pushing the Ministry of Transport to approve the construction of the bypass for decades. In March 2022, Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon wrote an open letter to Minister of Transport François Bonnardel to express the need for the bypass.

"Vaudreuil-Dorion region is THE link for trade and transit between Quebec and Ontario," Pilon wrote in his letter to Bonnardel.

Pilon also framed the need for the bypass as a safety issue, as the volume of traffic makes it difficult for pedestrians, especially children, to navigate the area.

"We are constantly one serious accident away from a tragedy," Pilon warned in his letter.

Vaudreuil-Soulanges MNA Marie-Claude Nichols also voiced her support to build a Highway 20 bypass in June 2021 after the Île aux Tourtes Bridge was forced to close, which created a traffic bottleneck along the stretch of the Highway that passes through Harwood and Île Perrot.

MTQ recently commenced a study of the area with a firm specialized in traffic and urban planning to propose possible solutions. But Van Neste estimates that this study could take two years.

"We are confident that this study will lead to interesting proposals for the Highway 20 bypass," Van Neste said.
