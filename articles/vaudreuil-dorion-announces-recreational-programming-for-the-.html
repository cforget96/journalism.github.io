Vaudreuil-Dorion announces recreational programming for the summer

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jun 22, 2022

Next weekend, Vaudreuil-Dorion's annual Circus Festival will kick off an array of recreational and cultural events for the summer season.

From June 23 to 25, the Circus will offer a variety of rides, street entertainment and live music on Jeannotte Street and St. Charles Avenue.

On the June 24, musical spectacle group Quebec Issime will take the Harden Stage on Jeannotte Street at 7:30 p.m. before the Saint-Jean-Baptiste fireworks on Vaudreuil Bay at 10. Quebec rock stars Les Cowboys Frignants will close out the festival at 8 p.m. on Saturday night.

Vaudreuil-Dorion will also be spotlighting its award-winning 405 park this summer. In the park's second season, the city will offer adult "Carte Blanche" workshops including terrarium creation, watercolor painting, wine glass painting and more. Registration begins on July 1.

The 405 will be hosting a number of events for all ages throughout the summer, including live music, stand-up comedy in collaboration with Just For Laughs, and outdoor movie screenings.

A number of events will also be hosted at the Maison-Valois park including the "Rhythmes d'Été" live music events every Tuesday starting on July 5, and the "Éveil" exhibition, which features three-dimensional art inspired by nature in bloom.

Lastly, the city's arts and cultural distributer, Mozaïk, will debut the "Mozaïk Caravan," a multipurpose bus that will be used as a mobile library, a traveling exhibition hall, a musical stage, and much more throughout the summer.
