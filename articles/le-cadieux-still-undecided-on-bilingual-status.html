Île-Cadieux still undecided on bilingual status

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Undated

Residents of the small Vaudreuil-Soulanges municipality will have to wait a bit longer to discover the fate of their town's bilingual status.

In May, the Legault government unveiled bill 96, which would revoke a municipality's bilingual status if census data showed that English was the first language of less than half of the municipality's population.

Many anglophones in the Montreal metropolitan area are concerned, as losing bilingual status would mean that cities would not be required to communicate with residents in English.

Municipalities will still be allowed to maintain their bilingual status, but they must vote to do so within 120 days of the bill's adoption.

In a written statement, Île-Cadieux Mayor Daniel Martel pointed out that the bill may still be amended before it is passed, so the city is waiting for it to become law before addressing the issue.

Martel also noted that the bill will likely not be passed before the town's municipal election, so the best decision will be to wait until after the election and allow the new administration to address the issue.

The Île-Cadieux municipal election will take place on November 7.
