Vaudreuil-Dorion features the performing arts with inaugural Errances festival

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Sep 7, 2022

Between Vaudreuil-Dorion's annual circus and events at The 405, there was no shortage of musical entertainment in Vaudreuil-Dorion this summer. But on September 16 and 17, the city will spotlight the performing arts with a new festival, Errances.

"This plan has been in the works since I began working here seven years ago," says Chloé Marcil, head of the culture and community life department of Vaudreuil-Dorion. "A number of performing arts will be represented at the festival, including dance, theatre, poetry readings and much more. The city does a lot with visual art and music, but there really isn't any other festival like this in the region."

Errances will be divided between five different spaces: the Vaudreuil-Dorion water tower, Maison Félix-Leclerc, Esther-Blondin park, Ile Bray park, and The 405, the latter being the central venue. The idea is to create a circuit-style festival that showcases lesser-known public areas.

"We want to get residents to explore their own city while also making it accessible," Marcil said. "Each performance will be about 15 minutes, so we'll have a bus system running to transport festival goers from one place to another."

The Quebec Ministry of Culture will finance 50 per-cent of the festival this year and in 2023, with performers from all over the province coming to take part in the event. The performances will cater to different audiences; for example, the performances on Saturday morning, will be oriented towards children.

Marcil said there was no headlining star at the event, but that the final performance at the 405 will be one you won't want to miss.

"We want the festival to be a lasting traditions for years to come. The city is very excited to share the festival with our citizens," she said.
