Domaine-en-Haut neighbourhood to get clean water after 12-year boil advisory

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 21, 2022

For most Vaudreuil-Dorion residents, having clean water is a given. But for the 34 residences of Domaine-en-Haut, clean tap water is something they've had to live without for the last 12 years. The small neighbourhood, located off the section Harwood Blvd. north of the Cite-des-Jeunes intersection, was put on water boil advisory after a high concentration of E.coli and chlorine were detected in the neighbourhood's artesian well, which was built in 1978.

"Even after you boil the water for a few minutes, it's still yellow," said resident Sally Nour. "It will be nice to finally be able to drink water from the tap."

Now, the Ministry of Municipal Affairs and Housing has agreed to grant an amount of $1,744,633, or 85% of the cost of work, for a new wastewater treatment system, and $912,350, or 50% of eligible costs, for a drinking water supply pipe that will connect the Domaine-en-Haut sector to city aqueduct network and filtration plant. The rest of the cost will be covered by the city.

"It has already been eight years since the City began working with the government to resolve the problem of drinking water and wastewater in the Domaine-en-Haut sector," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "It is therefore a relief for us to be able to undertake this work which will have an incredible impact on the quality of life of the residents of this sector. They will finally be served by quality drinking water and will have the assurance that the treatment of their wastewater will comply with the environmental standards in force."

While the project may sound like good news for the residents of Domaine-en-Haut, the celebration is not unanimous. The area has been on water boil advisory for so long because of a lack of public funding, but also because the residents refused to pay additional taxes for the project. The city proposed a project to the residents when the advisory first came into effect in 2010, but they filed a petition in opposition to it.

According letter from the city obtained by The 1019 Report, residents of Domaine-en-Haut will carry 74 per cent of the tax burden for the project, while the rest of the city will cover the remaining 26 per cent. That means a Domaine-en-Haut resident with a property valuation of $200,000 would have to pay an additional $1,650 per year in taxes over a 30-year period beginning in 2024.

"The cost to link the neighbourhood to the rest of the city's water system is particularly high because it is located in such a remote area," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Director General Olivier Van Neste.

For some residents, the tax increase might force them to move out of the area.

"I've been spending about $300 a year on bottled water," said Domaine-en-Haut resident Adam Krawczyk. "The taxes are more than triple that. I'm sure some people will leave for that reason."

Van Neste added that the project has been completely approved, so work should begin in the coming weeks and will continue into 2023.
