Vaudreuil-Dorion to extend bike paths on its two biggest commercial boulevards

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Nov 24, 2021

Vaudreuil-Dorion plans to add to its whopping 56.7km of bike paths in the next year. The funding for the bike paths will come from the Quebec Ministry of Transportation and Trame Verte et Bleue, a network run by the Greater Metropolitan Region of Montreal that provides financial aid to recreational and tourism projects that enhance natural environments.

The aid will be put toward three separate projects, two of which will incorporate the town's major commercial arteries into its impressive network of bike paths. An interactive map of the paths are available on the city's website.

The first project will be to build a path along the north side of de la Gare Blvd. between Dollarama and Metro. The city projects the cost to be approximately $150,000, 85 per cent of which will be financed by the Ministry of Transportation and the Greater Metropolitan Region of Montreal.

The move confirms the city's plan to make the commercial boulevard pedestrian-friendly, which will be useful once the town's Municipal Hub project comes to fruition. The Hub, whose construction has been put on hold due to market overheating, will eventually include a new City Hall, a library, a public square and an aquatic complex. 51 per cent of housing in Vaudreuil-Dorion is located less than 2 km from the site and 95 per cent is located at a distance of less than 4 km, making pedestrian and bicycle traffic essential the future Hub.

A separate project, which is still being planned, will connect the de la Gare bike path to the north side of Cité-des-Jeunes Blvd. and will lead to the future site of the Vaudreuil-Soulanges hospital. Although the hospital is only projected to be ready in 2026, the move will make the hospital site accessible to people without cars.

The city is also applying for financing to repair Besner linear park, a long stretch of bike path that runs parallel to St. Charles Ave. behind the Jean Coutu. The project is evaluated at nearly $375,000 and will be completed in 2022 barring delays.

As Vaudreuil-Dorion continues to grow, the city will rely on its bike paths even more, not only for cyclists, but to ensure the safety of children coming to and from school, for instance. Mayor Guy Pilon cites the development of cyclable paths as one of his proudest achievements over his 15-year tenure.

"The more than 50 bicycle paths in Vaudreuil-Dorion has made our city safer and more accessible. I'd like to applaud our administration for the good work they've done," Pilon said.
