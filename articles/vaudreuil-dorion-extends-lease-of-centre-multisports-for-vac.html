Vaudreuil-Dorion extends lease of Centre Multisports for vaccinations until May 2022

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Dec 22, 2021

While locations for booster shots remain uncertain in other regions, Vaudreuil-Dorion has secured its vaccination centre for another five months. The Centre Multisports, located on de la Gare Blvd., will continue to be the central point of vaccination for residents in the Vaudreuil-Soulanges region.

The CISSS of Western Montérégie (CISSSMO) will continue to rent the space from the town for $9,000 a month. The original lease spanned from March 1 to December 31, 2021.

The lease is a welcome source of income for the city as the Centre Multisports lost a significant amount of money due to closure over the COVID-19 pandemic. However, the income does not make up for all the money lost.

"The money the town received for renting the space helped to mitigate the losses, but the city still had to pay over $100,000 to the Centre Multisports to make up for the closure," explained Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon.

The Centre Multisports is an optimal site because of its location next to major thoroughfares and its accessibility by bus or train.

Vaccinations are being offered to adults receiving their first or second dose. Booster shots are currently available to people with weakened immune systems and people over 70 years old. The vaccine is also being offered to children between the ages of five and eleven.
