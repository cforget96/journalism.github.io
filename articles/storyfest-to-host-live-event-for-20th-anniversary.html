StoryFest to host live event for 20th anniversary

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Undated

Hudson's literary festival will host two award-winning authors at Greenwood Centre for Living History on August 28. The event will be the festival's first live event since 2019.

StoryFest will offer three presentations geared to different audiences. The first will take place at 10 a.m. with Marie-Louise Gay, a recipient of two Governor Genral's Awards for her books for children. She is best known for her Stella and Sam series, which has been adapted to television.

The second event at 1 p.m. will feature both Gay and her partner David Homel, an award-winning novelist, screenwriter, journalist and translator. Together, they have written five books in their "travels" series for young readers.

The final event will begin at 4 p.m. with Homel. His recent memoir on aging, Lunging into the Underbrush: A Life Lived Backward, he has called the battle between Eros and Melancholy. He is hoping the former wins.

All presentations require reservations. Tickets are $15 and include wine and cheese.
