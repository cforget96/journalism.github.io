Vaudreuil-Dorion to plant 10,000 trees per year for Two Billion Trees program

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Feb 16, 2022

As part of a concerted effort to foster green spaces in Vaudreuil-Dorion, the city is preparing a grant application for Natural Resources Canada's Two Billion Trees commitment.

The Two Billion Trees project will be accomplished over ten years as part of the Canadian government's goal of attaining net-zero greenhouse gas emissions by 2050. The project aims to reduce greenhouse gas emissions, to enhance biodiversity, and to support human well-being by creating more green sanctuaries in urban and suburban spaces.

"We must plant 10,000 trees to qualify for this program. We haven't calculated the exact costs yet, but we estimate that it may be approximately $450,000, 50% of which will be covered by Natural Resources Canada if we are approved," said town Director General Olivier Van Neste during Vaudreuil-Dorion's city council meeting on Feb. 7.

Van Neste added that a variety of trees will be planted across the municipality, ranging from saplings to mature trees. The city will plant the trees on a total area of approximately 9.5 hectares spread over at least ten different sites. Six of the sites will be near urban cores, which have higher population densities.
