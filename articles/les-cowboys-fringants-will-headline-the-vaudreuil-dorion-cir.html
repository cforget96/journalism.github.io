Les Cowboys Fringants will headline the Vaudreuil-Dorion Circus Festival this summer

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Apr 27, 2022

After a two-year hiatus, the Vaudreuil-Dorion Circus Festival is returning with a bang for its 16th edition on June 23, 24, and 25. And none other than award-winning folk-rock group Les Cowboys Fringants will rock Jeannotte Street on the final day of the festival.

The band will stop in Vaudreuil-Dorion during their tour of Quebec and French-speaking Europe to promote their new album, Les Antipodes. They were named group of the year at last year's ADISQ gala, an event that spotlights the independent music industry in Quebec.

"We are very proud to offer this show to our citizens. Les Cowboys Fringants were supposed to be part of the 2020 program, which was cancelled due to COVID-19," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Mayor Guy Pilon. "Anyone who attended their performance in 2018 can recall the festive atmosphere. A crowd of 7,000 people singing 'Les étoiles filantes' in unison brings back a few chills and unforgettable memories!"

The 2022 edition of the Circus Festival will feature free shows, street entertainment and various rides. The complete program will be unveiled at the end of May.
