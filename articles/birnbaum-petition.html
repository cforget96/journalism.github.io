Birnbaum petition

By Christopher Forget · Dec 8, 2021

As a native anglophone Quebecer, David Birnbaum sees the value and necessity of fortifying French language laws in the province. As Liberal MNA for D'Arcy McGee and Official Opposition Critic for Relations with English-Speaking Quebecers, he is concerned that the fundamental rights of the anglophone population is not being afforded the same degree of care as their francophone counterparts.

"I believe that every leader of Quebec must embrace the obligation to support and secure the rights of anglophones and francophones. Right now, the CAQ government doesn't seem to be paying much attention to the importance of this balance," Birnbaum said in an interview with The 1019 Report.

For this reason, Birnbaum has put forth a petition that demands that English-speaking Quebecers maintain the right to receive health and social services in their mother tongue.

In a recent move, the CAQ government disbanded the Provincial advisory committee that was responsible for English health care services. Quebec Minister of Health and Social Services Christian Dubé vows to create a new committee, but Birnbaum believes the decision puts a 35-year-long compromise at risk.

"This historic legal compromise---which has been upheld by Quebec premiers from Pariseau to Couillard---is entrenched in the Quebec Language Charter. It has become a linchpin that ensures an English-speaking pregnant woman in Gaspé can have the details of a cesarean operation explained to her in English, or that a teen struggling with addiction in Orford can speak with a social worker in his preferred language. The process of identifying these services was completed by this committee, which is why I proposed this petition," Birnbaum explained.

While Birnbaum noted that a valid reason for disbanding the committee was to better account for the needs of the First Nations and Inuit communities, he believes that this could have been accomplished without clearing house.

As of Dec. 1, the petition has amassed over 3,600 signatures. If you are interested in adding your signature to the petition, you can do so by visiting assnat.qc.ca and selecting the "Voice your opinion!" tab. From there, click on "Starting, Signing or Viewing a Petition," then "Signing an E-petition." Birnbaum's petition, which is called "Safeguarding the Right of Access to Health and Social Services in English," will be discoverable from there. The petition will be open until Dec. 8.
