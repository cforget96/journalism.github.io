1019 by the numbers: Photo radar infractions spiked after Île-aux-Tourtes Bridge closure

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 15, 2021

All motorists in Vaudreuil-Soulanges who regularly use the Île aux Tourtes Bridge remember when the provincial government abruptly closed the span for emergency repairs this spring. But a large subset of them remember what came later, when the bridge reopened: the photo radar trap.

After the bridge was closed from May 20th to May 31st, the Ministry of Transportation installed a photo radar that yielded 766 infractions in the month of June, despite the bridge only being fully reopened on June 21st. Then, the photo radar recorded a whopping 1713 infractions in July. In only a two-month period, $757,220 worth of tickets were issued from this photo radar trap alone.

For comparison, the permanent photo radar in Pincourt saw 1080 infractions in June and 1002 in July. The uptick of infractions at the Pincourt radar may have been a result of traffic being rerouted to Highway 20 during the bridge closure.

"Although I know some people believe the opposite, these are not money-making machines," said Gilles Payer, a Ministry of Transportation spokesperson. "We should always keep in mind that these machines exist to make areas safer, and they actually work: over time, people begin lowering their speed when they cross a radar zone."

The photo radar was installed on Highway 40 when the east side of the bridge went under construction. While there may be a myriad of reasons for the spike in infractions over the two-month period, the numbers at the Ile aux Tourtes radar location are still remarkably high.

"The numbers may have been rising for a number of reasons, such as weather or traffic. The construction holiday is also infamous for 'Operation Orange,' where the SQ runs a speed-watching blitz in construction zones. Finally, it could also simply be a case of people happening to drive fast in the same month," Payer told The 1019 Report.

Even if statistics suggest that photo radar traps make roads safer, for motorists who already had to endure the closure of the Île aux Tourtes Bridge, the speed trap tickets may feel like salt in the wound.
