Baie d'Urfe resident inducted into John Abbott Hall of Distinction

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Sep 14, 2022

In 2019, Frances Donald became the Chief Economist of Manulife Financial, making her the youngest Chief Economist in Canada and one of only two women to hold the title in the country. That same year, she was named to the list of Canada's Top 100 Most Powerful women by Women's Executive Network. She accomplished all of this at the age of 33.

That's why she's among the 11 JAC alumni to be inducted in the College's Hall of Distinction, a group of 50 people who were nominated to commemorate the 50 years since the college first opened in 1971.

Despite all of Donald's personal success, the Baie d'Urfe resident's most impressive trait may be her commitment to giving back to the communities that helped her break barriers.

"What amazes me about Frances, even now, several years after her time at John Abbott College, is the level of engagement she had during her studies here," explained Lison Desclos, Manager of the Communications and Foundations Offices at John Abbott College. "From student government to college committees to the Foundation, her contribution made such a difference in the lives of students who came after."

Donald spent her early years in the West Island, attending school in Beaconsfield. She graduated from John Abbott College in 2005 in Honours Social Science with a specialization in Math. In addition to her studies and committee work, Frances was also captain of the cheerleading team that won the silver medal at the 2004 provincial championships.

After Abbott, Donald obtained her Bachelor of Economics from Queen 's University in 2008. She worked as a research analyst for the Bank of Canada before attending New York University, where she earned her Master's in Economics in 2010.

After stints at Scotiabank, Deloitte and other financial institutions, Donald joined Manulife in 2016 as a senior economist before becoming Head of Macro-Economic Strategy in 2018.

Now, as the Chief Economist of Manulife Financial, Donald is has become a sought after commentator on global economic conditions by outlets such as CBC, CNBC, and BNN Bloomberg.

Of all the organizations Donald as been affiliated with, though, John Abbott will always occupy a special place in her life.

"In a recent conversation, Frances told me that she will always remember John Abbott College fondly and that it helped shape her future to what it is today," Desclos recalled.

The induction ceremony will take place in the Stewart Hall building at John Abbott on September 15. All 50 inductees into the Hall of Distinction were nominated by a committee of peers, including colleagues and former classmates. The College plans to induct one person into the Hall each year moving forward.

The other inductees this year include James Vanstone, a former history teacher and department chair of the History, Economics and Political Science departments; Andre Marceau, who worked as the campus bus driver and outdoor education assistant from 1977 to 2016; Jill Gowdey, and employee in the Student Activities department from 1978 to 2014; and alumni Annie Caron, who is now a freelance artist and retired soccer player; Darren Entwistle, now president and CEO of TELUs; Greg Foss, an executive director of Validus Power Corp; Phillip Grimm, an engineer with Palantir Technologies; Shahir Guindi, now national co-chair at the Toronto Law firm of Osler, Hoskin & Harcourt; Cynthia Kennedy, a vice-president of Incendo & Quebecor Content; and Bob McEwen, a partner at Cunningham's Pub and a longtime employee and volunteer at both John Abbott and Macdonald campuses.
