The 405 park survey reveals live music, themed nights were most popular events

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Nov 10, 2021

The results are in: The 405 park was a hit, and visitors want more live music next year. The park in Vaudreuil-Dorion has been advertised as a gathering destination that combines activities for all tastes. In the daytime, the space offers fitness classes and hammock rentals; by night, it becomes an open-air bar, concert hall, comedy club, and much more.

After its inaugural season, the survey showed that most visitors have a common love for live music in an outdoor ambiance. Nearly 40 per cent of all responders attended musical events, including DJ nights and a Thursday-night event that features upcoming musical artists.

Themed nights, including Oktoberfest, latin nights and country nights, were also very popular for the park, which is located across the street from Cité-des-Jeunes secondary school on St. Charles avenue.

"Based on the results of this survey, we can say without a doubt that The 405 has been a success. The comments are in line with those that our teams have heard on the ground over the past few months, " said Vincent Bastien, director of recreation and culture.

Of the 884 responses to the survey, nearly 50 per cent said they visited the park between two and five times. About 80 per cent of the responses were from Vaudreuil-Dorion residents; people from St. Lazare, Île Perrot and les Cèdres also contributed to the survey. Nearly 50 per cent of the responders were between the ages of 36 and 55.

While about a quarter of responders said they participated in the activities offered to children and families, daytime activities appeared to be less popular. Nearly 70 per cent of visitors did not rent the games or hammocks that were made available this past season. However, the purpose of the park is to give residents options.

"People have made the place their own. The 405 has proven to be the summer destination in Vaudreuil-Dorion," Bastien added.

The survey also asked visitors what they would like to see at the park next year. The most common suggestions were food trucks, musical shows featuring more known artists, additional parking for people with reduced mobility, and year-round programming.
