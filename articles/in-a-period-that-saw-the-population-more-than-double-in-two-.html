In a period that saw the population more than double in two decades, Guy Pilon's tenure as mayor has been one of change and development

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Oct 13, 2021

When you think of Vaudreuil-Dorion today, you might reflect on its fast pace of development and quick surge in population. For those who were part of that demographic growth, they might have been attracted by the thought of living in a new home or a modern condo tower. But for Guy Pilon, the man who has been the town's mayor since 2005, when the time comes to look back at all that change, he hopes his name will be mentioned.

"In the future, when questions are asked or when the history of the city is written about, they'll mention what our administration has done and what I've done, and that's enough to make me proud," Pilon said.

In fact, now that Pilon has been acclaimed to his fifth term as mayor, the history books will record that his tenure in the top office spanned a full 20 years -- a time when the city's rapid expansion routinely puts Vaudreuil-Dorion among the ranks of the fastest growing towns in Quebec.

The population surge over that period is substantial; according to the 2001 Canadian census, the city had 19,920 residents. In 2021, the Vaudreuil-Dorion city website states that the population is now 42,053, which is a 111% increase over two decades. By comparison, the Greater Montreal Area has only experienced a 23% increase over that span. Even within the Vaudreuil-Soulanges region, Vaudreuil-Dorion's population increase towers over municipalities like Pincourt or St. Lazare, who saw increases of 49% and 55%, respectively.

The city's population rise has influenced the decisions Pilon and his administration have had to make over the years. Based on the number of times he has been re-elected, it is clear that residents have been satisfied with his performance, families in particular.

The town was ranked the fourth-best city in Canada to raise a family in 2018 by MoneySense, an online personal finance magazine. Many projects Pilon and his team have worked on have transformed Vaudreuil-Dorion into a family-oriented municipality.

"There are so many projects I'm proud to have been a part of: the expansion of the Vaudreuil-Dorion arena, the more than 50 bicycle paths in the city, the 405 park, our environmental efforts, the multisport centre; all of these were accomplished by our administration," said Pilon in an interview with The 1019 Report.

Despite these accomplishments, Pilon's work is not finished. The city's strategic plan for the 2020-2025 term, which is available on the city's website, includes developing a green lung, turning the Quartier de la Gare into a community gathering place, and improving the city's public transit network.

The Vaudreuil-Soulanges hospital, a project that will change the landscape of the city, is set to open in 2026, one year after the end of Pilon's final term. Although he will not be in office to see the project through, Pilon knows he has made his mark.

"When the hospital project is finalized, I'll be happy because I know what I've done in my life and what I haven't done. I'll know my role very well. Even if I'm not there, everyone will know what was done during my tenure," he said.

Vaudreuil-Dorion is now the 30th largest city in Quebec in terms of population. Under Pilon's watch, the city has adapted to the population growth and developed into the well-run hub of the Vaudreuil-Soulanges region.

Once he completes his final term, Pilon says he intends to pursue his hobby of woodworking, a passion he developed in his youth working with his father. After two decades of public service, he has earned his time to rest.
