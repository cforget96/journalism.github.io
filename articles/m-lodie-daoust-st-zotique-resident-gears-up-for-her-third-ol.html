Mélodie Daoust: St. Zotique resident gears up for her third Olympic games

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Feb 2, 2022

Vaudreuil-Soulanges will have some homegrown talent representing Canada at the 2022 Winter Olympics in Beijing, her third time at the Games. Although Mélodie Daoust is technically a Valleyfield native, she is a St. Zotique resident and has been the town's official spokesperson since 2018, where she hopes to promote physical activity to residents of all ages.

Playing the forward position, Daoust is a well-decorated star and a key cog on Team Canada. She has won a gold and silver medal and was named Most Valuable Player in Women's Hockey at the 2018 games. In a recent list, TSN ranked her as the third-best player on the team, saying her play is defined by "three Ps: poise, patience, and production." She also made an appearance at the 2020 NHL All-Star Game alongside other top female players in an elite women's hockey showcase.

Her path to success was not unlike most elite female players. She grew up playing in the highest-level boys leagues before transitioning to the girls' game when she was 16. Later on, she was a member of the McGill Martlets for five seasons, where she was an integral part of the team's success.

She currently splits time playing for the Canadian Olympic team and coaching part-time for the Université de Montreal. She is also a mother to a three-year-old son with her partner, Audrey St-Germain.

Off the ice, Daoust has been at the forefront of the movement to grow women's hockey. When Canadian Women's Hockey League dissolved in 2019, it took her former team, the Montreal Canadiennes, along with it. Following the dissolution, she joined the Professional Women's Hockey Player's Association, a nonprofit organization dedicated to advocating for the promotion of professional women's hockey. After the CWHL folded, the only other professional women's league was the National Women's Hockey League, an organization for which many players have expressed dissatisfaction.

Although the state of professional women's hockey is still in a state of limbo, it is not for a lack of interest. On her Twitter page, Daoust recently retweeted that 315,000 Canadians tuned in to watch a women's game between Canada and the USA, which was more than any men's sporting event that evening.

Team Canada plays its first game of the Olympic Preliminary Round on Feb. 3 against Switzerland, where Daoust will continue to make Vaudreuil-Soulanges, and all of Canada, proud.
