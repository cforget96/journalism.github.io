West Island Cancer Wellness Centre launches website to help patients connect and inform

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET · Jun 22, 2022

At one point, everyone must deal with illness, whether it is one's own or caring for a friend or family member. But in addition to battling the illness, patients and caregivers are also tasked with an invisible job---keeping their loved ones in the loop. Communication can be unnecessarily burdensome, which is why the West Island Cancer Wellness Centre has come up with a solution: OnCourage.

The Wellness Centre, which is located off of St. Charles Blvd. north of Highway 40, recently launched OnCourage as a web-based communication service which allows patients and caregivers to share information related to their illness with friends and family.

"Some people use it as journal, some people use it to capture memories, others only use it to relay information," said Debbie Magwood, the Executive Director and founder of the Wellness Centre.

The idea for OnCourage came to Magwood when a friend was diagnosed with an aggressive form of cancer over the pandemic.

"She was overwhelmed by having to communicate with people reaching out to ask how she was doing," she said. "You have to repeat the same information over and over again, and often people want to help by giving food all at once, so you end up with a freezer full of food instead of having it spread out," she said.

Along with Maggie Costa, who handles fundraising for the Wellness Centre, Magwood was determined to find a solution to this problem.

"With OnCourage, you can make sure that everyone gets the same information. It's also secure---you can only access a webpage if the link has been shared with you," Magwood said.

The service is available Canada-wide, but since the website is still in its 3-month pilot project, the Wellness Centre doesn't have the data to see how much it's being used across the country.

The website has been adopted by a marketing company which has offered to support them pro-bono, so Magwood expects the awareness about the service to spread over the coming months.

"It brings everything together for people dealing with cancer, whether they're the patient or the caregiver," she said. "It makes things simpler."
