Vaudreuil-Dorion place of worship zoning by-law could go to referendum

By CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report · Apr 13, 2022

On April 4, the city of Vaudreuil-Dorion passed the second draft to amend a zoning bylaw that would allow religious organizations to use buildings in urban and commercial zones. Now, property owners in affected zones of the city will have a chance to oppose these amendments, which could lead to a referendum.

The city acknowledges that the amendment is long overdue. Controversy struck in 2020 when a number of Dorion residents suspected that 2400 Saint Antoine St., formerly Sunny's restaurant, was being used as an Islamic house of worship, which was illegal under municipal zoning laws.

"That was not the first time there has been an issue over the zoning of places of worship, but it showed we needed to address the issue," said Vaudreuil-Dorion Director General Olivier Van Neste.

Van Neste held a public consultation on March 28 to address questions about the amendment. Of the six residents in attendance, Van Neste said there was no resistance to the proposed zoning change.

"The residents who attended the consultation wanted to know more about which zones would be affected by the amendment. If there are people who want to oppose it, they were not present at the consultation," Van Neste said.

Vaudreuil-Dorion's efforts reflect a need to adapt to a shifting demographic, and the city recently received a grant of nearly $30,000 from the Ministry of Immigration, Francization and Integration to develop strategies to address needs of a culturally and religiously diverse population. According to Van Neste, the funds will be primarily used for consulting.

At present, the city does not have statistics to support the rise in diversity, but Van Neste says the changes are noticeable simply by going out in public.

"We see it every day, be it our library clientele, at the pool, at our events," Van Neste said. "The demand to make changes does not only come from residents, either; city employees are asking for us to make adjustments to to better serve the community."

Those interested in the specifics about which zones will be affected can consult the maps in the Public Notices section of the city's website. Property owners in the affected zones who want to oppose the amendments have until April 15 to submit the necessary forms, which are also available on the Public Notices page.
