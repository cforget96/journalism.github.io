Horse tale fundraiser

By Christopher Forget · Apr 27, 2022

Vaudreuil horse rescue centre holding fundraiser

to support its award-winning community work

CHRISTOPHER FORGET The 1019 Report

On May 7 and May 15 between 10 AM and 4 PM, A Horse Tale will be holding its third annual organic horse fertilizer and plant sale. 100 per cent of the proceeds will go directly to the centre, which cares for horses in need.

For the past nine years, AHT has offered experiences with horses to all members of the community, often visiting people in palliative care, people with disabilities, and women's shelters, to name a few of its initiatives.

"We give second chances to the horses, but we also give second chances to humans," A Horse Tale Executive Director Mike Grenier told The 1019 Report. AHT has been recognized at the national level by Horse Canada, winning the prestigious 'Hero of the Horse' award in 2019.

The fundraiser is a way for the organization, which is a volunteer-run nonprofit, to continue caring for the 14 horses currently living at the centre. Many of the horses come from the caleche industry, which was banned in Montreal in 2020.

Grenier invites all 1019 residents to "come out to the country, see the herd, see the volunteers."

For organizational purposes, the fundraiser is limited to about 100 cars a day. Those interested should register by email at manure@ahorsetale.org.
